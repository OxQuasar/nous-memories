# Shao Yong (邵雍, 1011–1077) — Reference Document

## Overview

Song Dynasty Neo-Confucian philosopher and cosmologist. Key figure in the xiangshu (象數, image-number) tradition, which treats the I Ching's mathematical structure as primary. His central claim: binary opposition (bifurcation) is the generative principle of everything — hexagrams, time, sound, and history are manifestations of one numerical structure at different scales.

---

## Core Principles

**Bifurcation (一分為二):** Taiji divides into yin/yang, each divides again into 4 images (四象), then 8 trigrams, then 64 hexagrams. A binary tree. The universe unfolds by repeated binary division.

**Xiantian (先天, Earlier Heaven):** The binary-counting arrangement attributed to Fu Xi. The hexagram's position IS its binary number. Structure is primary; meaning is read from structure. Distinguished from houtian (後天, Later Heaven / King Wen), which represents temporal manifestation.

**Ti/Yong (體/用):** Xiantian is ti (substance/essence) — the cosmological generation order. Houtian is yong (function/application) — how generated structure unfolds in time. Two phases of one process.

**Guanwu (觀物, Observing Things):** Observe things through their numerical pattern, not through subjective interpretation. "Use the eyes of all-under-heaven as one's own eyes." Numbers are not imposed on reality; they are reality's own structure made visible.

**Number generates image, image generates meaning** — not the reverse. This places Shao Yong in opposition to the yili (義理, meaning-principle) school of Cheng Yi.

## The Huangji Jingshi (皇極經世書)

"Supreme Principles Governing the World." Four major sections applying one principle (bifurcation) to time, sound, philosophy, and practice.

Two extant editions:
- Daozang (道藏) edition (Ming): 12 juan, 54 pian
- Siku Quanshu (四庫全書) edition (Qing): 14 juan, 64 pian (standard recension)

The Siku edition's 64-chapter structure mirrors its subject — the 64 hexagrams. Chapters 1–62 are titled 觀物篇 (Observing Things), numbered sequentially. Chapters 63–64 are the 觀物外篇 (Outer Chapters) compiled by disciples.

### Section I: Cosmological-Historical Timeline (Vols 1–6, Ch 1–34)

The temporal cycle system. Same historical span layered at different resolutions — not a partition of time but a stack of views.

**Vols 1–2 (Ch 1–12): 以元經會 — Regulating Hui through Yuan**
Coordinate grid at Shi resolution (30-year slots). 4,320 entries per volume (12 Hui × 30 Yun × 12 Shi). Four-coordinate addresses using Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches. Vol 1 (Hui 1–6) is empty — pre-civilization, the grid exists but nothing fills it. Vol 2 (Hui 7–12) has ruler/dynasty names in the slots, from 夏禹 (Yu of Xia) through Song. The structure precedes its content.

**Vols 3–4 (Ch 13–24): 以會經運 — Regulating Yun through Hui**
Year-level sexagenary listings. Vol 3 is mostly structural — starts at Hui 3 (寅) with the cosmogonic note "開物始月寅之中" (things begin to open). Vol 4 is dense with multi-state parallel chronicles: Spring and Autumn through end of recorded history. Multiple states (Zhou, Jin, Qi, Song, Chu, Qin, Lu) tracked simultaneously per year.

**Vols 5–6 (Ch 25–34): 以運經世 — Regulating Shi through Yun**
Year-level narrative entries. Vol 5 is sparse — only landmark events (first entry: Emperor Yao establishing the calendar). Vol 6 is dense — Warring States through Song, nearly every year has events. The content fills in as history densifies.

The temporal section layers the same period at increasing content density: empty grid → names → year-tables → narratives. Not sequential but stereoscopic.

### Section II: Sound and Phonology (Vols 7–10, Ch 35–50)

**聲音律品 — Sound and Phonology**

A 4×4 matrix of 16 chapters. 聲 (differentiated by Heaven's axes: 日月星辰) = the initiating/opening phase of a syllable. 音 (differentiated by Earth's axes: 水火土石) = the resolving/closing phase. Not cleanly consonant vs vowel in the modern sense — onset and completion of a sound event.

| 聲 ↓ \ 音 → | 水 Water | 火 Fire | 土 Soil | 石 Stone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **日 Sun** (V7) | Ch 35 | Ch 36 | Ch 37 | Ch 38 |
| **月 Moon** (V8) | Ch 39 | Ch 40 | Ch 41 | Ch 42 |
| **星 Stars** (V9) | Ch 43 | Ch 44 | Ch 45 | Ch 46 |
| **辰 Celestials** (V10) | Ch 47 | Ch 48 | Ch 49 | Ch 50 |

Each chapter further subdivided by tonal quality: 闢/翕 (opening/closing) for 聲, 清/濁 (clear/turbid) for 音. Columns alternate: odd chapters = 闢/清, even chapters = 翕/濁. Each cell contains the full character inventory for that phonological intersection, organized by the 10 Stems (cycling through onset groups) and 12 Branches (cycling through completion groups).

The same bifurcation principle that structures time now structures sound: Heaven differentiates the beginning, Earth differentiates the ending. The syllable as a microcosm of the creative cycle — 唱 (lead/initiate) and 和 (harmonize/respond).

### Section III: Inner Chapters — Philosophy (Vols 11–12, Ch 51–62)

The theoretical heart. Shao Yong's own systematic prose — continuous philosophical argument, not tables.

**Vol 11 (Ch 51–56):**
- **Ch 51:** The cosmological cascade. Heaven = dynamic (陽/陰), Earth = static (剛/柔). Bifurcation produces 太陽→日, 太陰→月, 少陽→星, 少陰→辰. Interactions of 暑寒晝夜 (heat/cold/day/night) and 雨風露雷 (rain/wind/dew/thunder) generate all natural phenomena. Four categories of life: 走飛草木 (walking, flying, grass, wood). Humans are "spiritual among the ten thousand things" because they receive all four sensory capacities.
- **Ch 52:** Ti/Yong of perception. Senses = 用 (function), sensory qualities = 體 (substance). The sage as 人之人 — "the person of persons" who observes all through one heart-mind.
- **Ch 53:** 窮理盡性以至于命. Dao → Heaven → Earth → Myriad Things → Humans. Heaven's four treasuries = four seasons; the Sage's four treasuries = 易書詩春秋 (the Four Classics).
- **Ch 54:** The 4×4 matrix of classics × governance modes. 易之易 = 生生 through 春秋之春秋 = 藏藏. Maps onto historical eras (三皇/五帝/三王/五伯) and specific rulers (文王/武王/周公/召公). Each cell = a quality × a mode.
- **Ch 55:** Four governance modes: 道/德/功/力 (Dao, Virtue, Merit, Force) — a fourfold moral degeneration from pristine to coercive. Four types of mandate (正命/受命/改命/攝命). Confucius transcends all four as the "ten-thousand-generation" teacher.
- **Ch 56:** Historical commentary. Confucius's editing of the classics as the fourfold mapping in action. Specific judgments on dynasties and states.

**Vol 12 (Ch 57–62):**
- **Ch 57–58:** Historical ethics. Grading rulers: Shun > Wu Wang > Huan Gong. Han between Huan and Wu. The 好生/好殺 axis — Zhou/Han chose life through 義, Qin/Chu chose death through 利.
- **Ch 59:** "From extreme disorder to extreme order requires three transformations." 皇=spring, 帝=summer, 王=autumn, 伯=winter. Correct vs incorrect Dao defined by role-consistency (君行君事, 臣行臣事).
- **Ch 60:** The 4×4 temporal recursion made explicit. 日=元, 月=會, 星=運, 辰=世, all 16 combinations with exact numbers (世之世 = 18,662,400). Parallel: 皇之皇 through 伯之伯 = 道/德/功/力 crossed with itself. Historical dynasties rated on this grid.
- **Ch 61:** Quantitative backbone. Body numbers vs use numbers: 太陽/少陽/太剛/少剛 body=160, use=112; 太陰/少陰/太柔/少柔 body=192, use=152. The 唱和 (singing/harmonizing) operation produces 動數 and 植數 = 17,024 each.
- **Ch 62:** Bifurcation applied to taxonomy. 4×4 classification of things (飛飛 through 草草) and people (士士 through 商商), mapped to 性情形體 and 仁禮義智 respectively. Relative abundances at 1:10:100:1000 ratios. Culminates in 反觀 — "observing things through things, not through the self" — the epistemological summit.

### Section IV: Outer Chapters — Disciples' Records (Vols 13–14)

Compiled by disciples (traditionally attributed to son Shao Bowen 邵伯溫 and students). Fragments, aphorisms, and collected sayings — not systematic treatises but a master's teachings as recorded by listeners.

**Vol 13 (觀物外篇上):**
- Yi numerology: body vs use numbers, yarrow stalk mathematics (大衍之數), the round/square distinction (圓/方). Round = Heaven's operation (grows by 6), square = Earth's generation (grows by 8).
- Hexagram structure: 4 正卦 (乾坤坎離) that don't reverse, 28 反易 pairs → 36 archetypes. The bifurcation principle in its purest form: 一分為二, 二分為四, 四分為八.
- Xiantian diagram: "先天圖者環中也" — the center of the ring. "先天學心法也" — xiantian learning is the method of the heart-mind. "堯之前先天也堯之後後天也" — before Yao is xiantian, after Yao is houtian.
- Historical judgments: Five Hegemons ranked, Spring and Autumn commentary. Confucius's 四絕 (毋意毋必毋固毋我).

**Vol 14 (觀物外篇下):**
- Metaphysics: 太極 as both heart-mind and Dao. "心為太極又曰道為太極." Generation: 太極→神→數→象→器. Everything has its own taiji.
- Yin-yang aphorisms: yang rises/yin descends, yang knows/yin cannot know, yang round/yin square. "陰事太半蓋陽一而陰二."
- Astronomy: solstices, equinoxes, eclipses, the Northern Dipper. "月體本黑受日之光故白" — the moon is inherently dark, white only by reflected sunlight.
- Natural taxonomy: flying/walking/grass/wood crossed with habitat/diet. Humans among the walking class. Land and water creatures mirror inversely.
- Body-cosmos mapping: organs to trigrams (乾=heart, 兌=spleen, 離=gallbladder, 震=kidney, etc.). Heart and gallbladder hang inverted because humans grow opposite to plants.
- Ethics: "以物觀物性也以我觀物情也" — objectivity through things vs subjectivity through self. "心一而不分則能應萬變" — an undivided heart responds to ten thousand changes.
- Appendix: 四庫全書 editorial note on Zhang Xingcheng's 皇極經世索隱 (Song-dynasty commentary). Qianlong-era addition.

## The Yuan-Hui-Yun-Shi Cycle System

Nested temporal cycles derived from the same numbers that generate hexagrams. A fractal analogy to the ordinary calendar:

| Cosmic Unit | Chinese | Analogous To | Duration | Count per Parent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Yuan 元 | Great Year (大年) | Year | 129,600 years | 1 |
| Hui 會 | Great Month (大月) | Month | 10,800 years | 12 per Yuan |
| Yun 運 | Great Day (大日) | Day | 360 years | 30 per Hui |
| Shi 世 | Great Hour (大時辰) | Double-hour (時辰) | 30 years | 12 per Yun |

Derivation: 12 × 30 × 12 × 30 = 129,600. Equivalently, 360² = 129,600.

The 12 and 30 come from:
- 12: the 12 sovereign hexagrams (消息卦), the 12 Earthly Branches, the 12 months, the 12 double-hours
- 30: the days in a month

### The 12 Hui mapped to Earthly Branches

- Hui of Zi (子): Heaven emerges (天開於子)
- Hui of Chou (丑): Earth emerges (地闢於丑)
- Hui of Yin (寅): Humanity emerges (人生於寅)
- Remaining branches: civilization rises, peaks, declines
- Recorded human history (Yao onward) falls within the middle portion

### The 12 Sovereign Hexagrams (消息卦)

The macro-rhythm of the cycle — yang waxing and waning:

| Month | Hexagram | Yang Lines | Phase |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------|
| 11th | Fu 復 (Return) | 1 | Yang begins |
| 12th | Lin 臨 (Approach) | 2 | Yang grows |
| 1st | Tai 泰 (Peace) | 3 | Balance |
| 2nd | Da Zhuang 大壯 (Great Strength) | 4 | Yang dominates |
| 3rd | Guai 夬 (Breakthrough) | 5 | Yang nearly full |
| 4th | Qian 乾 (Heaven) | 6 | Pure yang — peak |
| 5th | Gou 姤 (Encounter) | 5 (1 yin) | Yin begins |
| 6th | Dun 遯 (Retreat) | 4 (2 yin) | Yin grows |
| 7th | Pi 否 (Stagnation) | 3 (3 yin) | Balance |
| 8th | Guan 觀 (Contemplation) | 2 (4 yin) | Yin dominates |
| 9th | Bo 剝 (Splitting Apart) | 1 (5 yin) | Yin nearly full |
| 10th | Kun 坤 (Earth) | 0 | Pure yin — nadir |

Each Hui in the Yuan cycle corresponds to one of these phases.

### Hexagram-to-History Mapping

- Uses the **xiantian (Fu Xi) sequence**, not King Wen
- Each Yuan, Hui, Yun, and Shi receives a governing hexagram
- Individual years also receive hexagram representations
- The binary-counting order IS the temporal order
- Historical scope documented: ~2357 BCE (Yao) through ~1077 CE (Northern Song)

### Astronomical Alignment

- 129,600 = 5 × 25,920 (five precession cycles at the traditional Platonic Year value)
- Base unit 360 = approximate days/year = degrees in a circle
- The 12-fold and 30-fold divisions mirror observable calendrical periods
- Whether the alignment with precession is intentional or a consequence of shared base numbers (12, 30, 360) is an open question
- Chinese astronomers knew about precession by Shao Yong's time (Yu Xi 虞喜, 4th century, estimated ~18,000 years)

## The Xiantian Diagrams

### Circular Diagram (圓圖)

64 hexagrams arranged in a circle. Qian (111111) at top, Kun (000000) at bottom. Right semicircle counts down in binary (63→32), left semicircle counts up (0→31). Diametrically opposite hexagrams are always complements (XOR = 111111). Represents Heaven — temporal/cyclical.

### Square Diagram (方圖)

64 hexagrams in an 8×8 grid. Upper trigram determines the row, lower trigram determines the column (or vice versa). Represents Earth — spatial/structural.

### Combined Square-within-Circle (方圓合一圖)

Square nested inside circle — the unity of temporal (Heaven/circular) and spatial (Earth/square) structure.

## Relation to Opposition Theory

Shao Yong's xiantian system is the purest expression of concentrated opposition:
- All diametric pairs are complement (maximum opposition strength)
- Sequential flow follows binary counting (smooth, algebraically structured)
- One principle (bifurcation) generates all structure
- Opposition is stored in static pairings, not expressed in flow

The King Wen system distributes the same opposition into dynamic process:
- Mixed pairing distances (reversal + complement)
- Varied sequential transitions (kernel diversity, anti-repetition)
- Multiple layered principles (matching, ordering, orientation)
- Opposition woven into flow

Shao Yong's own framework captures this: xiantian = ti (substance), houtian/KW = yong (function). The generation order concentrates opposition; the manifestation order distributes it.
