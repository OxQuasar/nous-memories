# Phase 2 Capstone: The Weight-Preservation Framework

## 1. The Characterization

**Theorem.** The King Wen hexagram pairing is the unique perfect matching of {0,1}⁶ that:

1. Is invariant under the mirror-pair partition group of B₆ (order 384)
2. Minimizes weight tilt (mean |Δw| across pairs)

No other pairing satisfies both conditions. Condition 1 admits exactly 9 pairings; condition 2 selects KW uniquely among them (WT = 0.375 vs ≥ 1.125 for all others).

Equivalently: KW is the unique pairing that respects the hexagram's positional symmetry and preserves yin-yang balance wherever the symmetry permits.

---

## 2. The Optimization Target and Constraint Set

### The constraint: mirror-pair invariance

The 6 line positions decompose into 3 mirror pairs: {L1↔L6, L2↔L5, L3↔L4}. The subgroup of B₆ (signed permutations on 6 coordinates, order 46,080) preserving this partition has order 384 = (Z₂ ≀ S₃) × (Z₂)³, generated by:

- 3 within-pair position swaps (L1↔L6, L2↔L5, L3↔L4)
- 3 pair-value flips (complement bits at each mirror pair independently)

This group is defined by positional geometry alone — no reference to any pairing.

**Theorem.** This 384-element group equals the stabilizer of the KW pairing in B₆. Verified by exhaustive check of all 46,080 elements of B₆.

Under this group, the 64 states collapse into 4 orbits (sizes 8, 8, 24, 24). The pair-orbit structure yields 8 valid pair-orbits (no internal state overlap). The two size-8 orbits each have a unique valid pair-orbit (forced). The two size-24 orbits each have 3 valid pair-orbits (reversal, complement, comp∘rev). Total: 1 × 1 × 3 × 3 = **9 equivariant pairings**.

### The target: weight preservation

Weight tilt (WT) = mean absolute yang-count difference across pairs: (1/32) Σᵢ |w(aᵢ) − w(bᵢ)|.

- Reversal preserves weight: w(rev(x)) = w(x), so Δw = 0.
- Complement inverts weight: w(comp(x)) = 6 − w(x), so Δw = |6 − 2w|.
- Comp∘rev inverts weight: same as complement.

For the size-24 orbits, reversal is the unique weight-preserving operation. For the size-8 orbits (palindromes and their complements), the pairing is forced to complement — reversal maps palindromes to themselves, not to a partner.

KW chooses reversal for both size-24 orbits (24 pairs, all Δw = 0) and accepts complement for the two size-8 orbits (8 pairs, Δw > 0). This uniquely minimizes WT.

| Pairing | Size-24 operations | S | D | WT |
|---------|-------------------|---|------|------|
| Complement | comp, comp | 192 | 0.00 | 1.875 |
| **KW** | **rev, rev** | **120** | **2.75** | **0.375** |
| KW's mirror | cr, cr | 120 | 2.75 | 1.875 |
| 6 mixed | various | 96–168 | 1.55–2.00 | 1.125–1.875 |

The WT gap between KW (0.375) and the next-best (1.125) is threefold. No secondary criterion is needed.

## 3. Uniqueness Results by Scale

### n = 6: Unique, single-criterion

KW is the unique WT-minimizer among 9 equivariant pairings. Weight preservation alone suffices — no lexicographic tiebreaker needed.

Among random pairings: 99.98th percentile in opposition strength. 0 of 10,000 random samples are even Z₂²-equivariant, let alone invariant under the full 384-element group.

### n = 5: Extreme but not uniquely minimal

32 states, 2 mirror pairs {L1↔L5, L2↔L4}, center L3. Stabilizer order 64 = (Z₂ ≀ S₂) × (Z₂)³ (center position has no permutation freedom). 3 state-orbits under the full stabilizer, 13 valid pair-orbits, **63 equivariant pairings**.

KW-style: S = 52, WT = 0.625, 99.7th percentile among random pairings. But WT = 0.625 is NOT the minimum — an equivariant pairing with WT = 0.25 exists (S = 36). At n = 5, weight preservation alone doesn't select KW-style; the characterization requires the lexicographic form: minimize WT, then maximize S.

The transition from n = 5 to n = 6: the WT gap widens from competitive (0.625 vs 0.25) to decisive (0.375 vs 1.125). At n = 6, the mirror-pair geometry is rich enough that weight preservation becomes a sharp selector.

### n = 4: Mediocre

16 states, 2 mirror pairs, 6 Z₂² orbits. KW-style is at the 75th percentile for strength — dominated by 23,632 of 2,027,025 pairings. Reversal at n = 4 produces only 2 mask types (too few for diversity). The mirror-pair vocabulary requires ≥ 3 independent pairs (n ≥ 6) to express fully.

117 Z₂²-equivariant pairings exist. The full stabilizer landscape was not computed — the orbit structure is too sparse to meaningfully constrain.

### n = 3: Divergent

8 states, 1 mirror pair {L1↔L3}, center L2. 9 equivariant pairings under Z₂². The traditional (Fu Xi / Shuo Gua) pairing is **complement**, not reversal.

A weight-preserving equivariant pairing exists at n = 3: reversal for the single size-4 orbit, complement for the palindromes. This gives S = 10, WT = 1.00. The tradition chose complement (S = 12, WT = 1.50) — prioritizing strength over weight preservation.

The divergence is genuine: n = 3 and n = 6 apply opposite priorities (strength >> WT at n = 3; WT >> strength at n = 6).

## 4. Cross-Scale Consistency Analysis

| n | States | |Stab| | Eq. pairings | KW S/S_max | KW S %ile | KW WT | Unique min WT? |
|---|--------|--------|--------------|------------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| 3 | 8 | — | 9 | 83% | ~99% | 1.00 | No |
| 4 | 16 | — | 117 | 75% | ~75% | — | — |
| 5 | 32 | 64 | 63 | 65% | 99.7% | 0.625 | No |
| 6 | 64 | 384 | 9 | 63% | 99.98% | 0.375 | **Yes** |

Three regimes:

1. **n = 3: Sparse geometry.** One mirror pair. Reversal and complement are not structurally distinguishable — both are valid equivariant operations, and weight preservation has no traction (even the weight-preserving pairing has WT = 1.00 because palindromes are forced to complement). Tradition chose the stronger operation.

2. **n = 4: Transitional.** Two mirror pairs. KW-style is equivariant but mediocre — the orbit structure is too uniform (all reversal pairs have Hamming distance 2) for reversal to generate diversity.

3. **n ≥ 5: Rich geometry.** KW-style is extreme (99.7th–99.98th percentile among random pairings). Weight preservation becomes the distinctive property. At n = 5 it's the leading criterion but needs S as tiebreaker. At n = 6 it's sufficient alone.

The number of equivariant pairings is non-monotone: 9 → 117 → 63 → 9. The stabilizer group grows with n (compressing the space), but inter-orbit pairing combinatorics grow at intermediate scales. At n = 6, the 384-element group is large enough to collapse everything to 9.

**Verdict:** The weight-preservation principle is structurally productive at n ≥ 5 and uniquely selective at n = 6. It does not explain the n = 3 tradition.

## 5. Sequential Variety: Separate Axis

kac (sequential anti-repetition of opposition types) does not integrate into the pairing characterization. The reasons are structural:

- The 9 equivariant pairings describe **which states are paired** — a static partition of {0,1}⁶.
- kac measures **how opposition types alternate along the sequence** — a dynamic, order-dependent property.
- kac requires the tradition-supplied sequence as input. It cannot be computed from the pairing alone.

The connection between the two layers: once the pairing fixes the mask vocabulary (7 signature masks for KW), kac measures how the sequence distributes that vocabulary across consecutive transitions. The pairing determines the alphabet; the sequence determines the grammar.

kac remains an independent structural property. Its integration requires extending the framework from the pairing layer to the sequence layer — Phase 3/4 territory.

## 6. Equivariance: Derived Property

Equivariance is not an imposed prior. It is a consequence of a more fundamental geometric fact.

The mirror-pair partition group is defined by the hexagram's positional structure: which line positions are equidistant from the figure's center. This group exists independently of any pairing. The theorem that it equals KW's stabilizer means:

- KW respects the positional geometry because it uses reversal (spatial inversion), which is defined by that geometry.
- The equivariance under Z₂² = {id, comp, rev, comp∘rev} — the property used throughout the discovery phase — is a consequence of invariance under the larger 384-element group.
- The Z₂² subgroup (index 96) was sufficient to discover the weight-preservation principle but far from the tightest constraint.

Operationally, equivariance means the pairing is a **law**, not a **list**: the two-clause rule "reverse it; if that's itself, complement it" generates all 32 pairs without referencing any specific hexagram. The rule is defined in terms of group operations, so invariance under those operations is automatic.

## 7. Predictions for Untested Cases

The framework makes three falsifiable predictions at any n ≥ 7:

1. **Stabilizer = mirror-pair partition group.** The KW-style pairing's stabilizer in Bₙ will equal the subgroup preserving the mirror-pair partition {L1↔Lₙ, L2↔Lₙ₋₁, ...}.

2. **Extreme opposition strength.** KW-style will occupy a high percentile (≥ 99th) among random pairings for opposition strength.

3. **Weight-preserving and (near-)unique.** KW-style will be the unique or near-unique WT-minimizer among equivariant pairings under the full stabilizer.

**The parity conjecture.** The n = 5 vs n = 6 comparison suggests:
- At even n, the WT gap between KW-style and the next-best equivariant pairing is clean (threefold at n = 6). Weight preservation alone suffices.
- At odd n, a center line reduces symmetry, inter-orbit pairing introduces alternatives, and the characterization may require the lexicographic form (WT >> S).

This is testable at n = 7 (128 states, |B₇| = 645,120) and n = 8 (256 states).

## 8. Implications for Phase 3 and Phase 4

### Phase 3: Nuclear trigrams and the L3|L4 membrane

Phase 2 established that the mirror-pair decomposition {L1↔L6, L2↔L5, L3↔L4} is the geometric prior for the KW pairing. The nuclear trigram decomposition {L2-L3-L4, L3-L4-L5} cuts across mirror pairs, straddling the L3|L4 boundary — the site of maximal non-alignment between mirror-pair geometry and trigram geometry.

Phase 2 tells us exactly what the mirror-pair geometry determines (the pairing) and what it doesn't (the sequence, the nuclear structure). The Phase 3 question: does the L3|L4 membrane carry opposition information that neither decomposition captures alone?

### Phase 4: 生克 synthesis

Phase 2 provides three structural anchors for Phase 4:

1. **The 9-pairing landscape** gives a finite, complete context for KW's structural position. Any claim about KW's design logic must address why it occupies the weight-preserving vertex rather than any of the other 8.

2. **Weight preservation** (yin-yang balance conserved under pairing) connects directly to five-phase theory where elemental balance is a primary concern.

3. **Orientation within pairs** — the 体/用 assignment, the 27 load-bearing bits, the developmental priority — describes choices *after* the pairing is fixed. These are exactly the degrees of freedom Phase 2 identifies but does not characterize: the sequence and ordering layer.

Phase 3 (nuclear structure) and Phase 4 (生克) are independent and can proceed in parallel. Neither requires the other as prerequisite.

## 9. Open Questions

### Structural (within the framework)

1. **Cross-scale divergence (Q3).** The tradition applies opposite principles at n = 3 (strength >> WT) and n = 6 (WT >> strength). Does a single deeper principle generate both choices? The most promising direction: "maximize the opposition structure that the available geometry can distinguish" — which is complement at n = 3 (where geometry is trivial) and reversal at n ≥ 5 (where geometry is rich). But this is unformalized and speculative.

2. **The parity conjecture.** Is the lexicographic complication at odd n (n = 5 needs WT >> S; n = 6 needs only WT) a general pattern? Testable at n = 7, 8.

3. **Algebraic ceiling at general n.** KW at n = 6 achieves D = 2.75 = 98% of log₂7. Does the reversal-priority principle produce near-ceiling diversity at all n? The ceiling is log₂(2^⌊n/2⌋ − 1) — the number of nonzero signature masks.

### Extending the framework

4. **Sequential layer (Q5).** kac measures how opposition types distribute across the sequence. The pairing framework addresses what is paired; the sequence framework must address how pairs are ordered and oriented. These are different structural layers with potentially different organizing principles.

5. **Nuclear trigram boundary (Phase 3).** The mirror-pair partition and the trigram partition are two incompatible decompositions of 6 positions. Their non-alignment at L3|L4 may carry structural information visible only at the intersection.

6. **Orientation and ordering (Phases 3–4).** The 27 load-bearing bits, M-score, asymmetry, and 体/用 assignment are all post-pairing degrees of freedom. These are the remaining three of four structural layers identified in the KW studies. The pairing layer is now characterized; the other three are not.

### Epistemological

7. **Selection criterion vs. generative principle.** The framework uniquely identifies KW as a structural position. Whether it describes the historical design logic — whether the tradition *intended* weight preservation — is a separate question that the mathematical framework cannot resolve. The characterization describes where KW sits, not how it got there.
