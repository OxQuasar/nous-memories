# n=6 Opposition Measures — Results

## Setup

64 six-bit states, partitioned into 32 pairs. Exhaustive enumeration impossible (~10⁴⁴ pairings). Instead: exact measures for two named pairings + 100K random sample for distribution context.

### Z₂² orbit structure at n=6

Group {id, reversal, complement, comp∘rev} acting on {0,...,63}:
- **20 orbits**: 8 of size 2, 12 of size 4
- **8 palindromes** (reversal-fixed): {000000, 001100, 010010, 011110, 100001, 101101, 110011, 111111}
- **8 comp∘rev-fixed** (anti-palindromes): {000111, 001011, 010101, 011001, 100110, 101010, 110100, 111000}
- **0 complement-fixed** (none at even n)

Both reversal and comp∘rev have 8 fixed points each — neither yields a valid complete pairing alone. Same structural constraint as n=4.

### KW pairing structure

32 pairs decompose as:
- **4 palindrome complement pairs** — distance 6, mask 111111, Δweight ∈ {2, 6}
- **4 "accidental" complement pairs** — non-palindromic states where rev(x) = comp(x) (comp∘rev-fixed). Distance 6, mask 111111, Δweight = 0
- **24 pure reversal pairs** — distance ∈ {2, 4}, weight preserved

**Hamming distance distribution:**
| Distance | Count | Source |
|----------|-------|--------|
| 2 | 12 | reversal (1 mirror pair differs) |
| 4 | 12 | reversal (2 mirror pairs differ) |
| 6 | 8 | complement (all 3 mirror pairs differ) |

**XOR mask distribution (7 distinct masks from 63 possible):**
| Mask | Distance | Count |
|------|----------|-------|
| 111111 | 6 | 8 |
| 100001 | 2 | 4 |
| 010010 | 2 | 4 |
| 001100 | 2 | 4 |
| 110011 | 4 | 4 |
| 101101 | 4 | 4 |
| 011110 | 4 | 4 |

The 7 masks are exactly the 7 nonzero elements of Z₂³ generated by the three mirror-pair indicator bits: O (outer, L1↔L6), M (middle, L2↔L5), I (inner, L3↔L4). Each mask appears in exactly 4 pairs. This is the algebraic structure identified in the KW studies: mask = orbit signature.

The distribution across masks is **perfectly uniform** (4 each for the 7 masks, except 111111 which gets 8 = 4 + 4 accidental). The diversity measure H = 2.750 rather than the maximum log₂(32) = 5.0 because there are only 7 types, and one type has double weight.

### Shao Yong pairing

All 32 pairs are complement. Single mask 111111. Maximum strength (192 = 6 × 32), zero diversity. Perfect weight anti-correlation (r = −1). The extreme of concentrated opposition at n=6.

### KW is extreme on every axis

The KW pairing is not in the interior of the random distribution — it's at the boundary or beyond on every measure:

- **Strength 99.98%**: Only 2 of 100K random pairings exceeded it. KW is near the top of achievable strength while maintaining algebraic structure.
- **Diversity 0.00%**: Below the entire 100K sample. Random pairings naturally spread across many mask types (D ≈ 4.5 vs KW's 2.75). The algebraic constraint (mask = orbit signature) forces KW to use only 7 of 63 possible masks.
- **Weight Tilt 0.00%**: Also below entire sample. Reversal pairs preserve weight (Δw = 0 for 28 of 32 pairs). Random pairings typically mismatch weights.
- **Weight Corr 97.65%**: Strongly positive — reversal preserves yang-count, so paired states tend to have similar weights. Random pairings average around +0.19.

## Comparison with n=4 Findings

### S↔D orthogonality persists

| Scale | Pearson r(S,D) | Method |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| n=3 | 0.000 (exact) | Exhaustive (105 pairings) |
| n=4 | 0.000 (exact) | Exhaustive (2,027,025 pairings) |
| n=6 | −0.002 (≈0) | Sample (100,000 pairings) |

The orthogonality is structural and scale-invariant. Strength and diversity are independent degrees of freedom at every scale tested.

### KW shifts from dominated to extreme

| Property | n=4 | n=6 |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Strength percentile | 75th | **99.98th** |
| Diversity percentile | — (dominated) | **0th** (below all random) |
| Weight Tilt percentile | minimum | **0th** |
| On any Pareto frontier | No | N/A (sampling, but extreme S) |
| Equivariant | Yes (1 of 117) | Yes (0 of 10K random) |

At n=4, the KW-style pairing was mediocre — moderate strength, moderate diversity, dominated by thousands of pairings. At n=6, it's extreme: near-maximum strength combined with unusually structured (low-entropy) mask distribution.

The key difference: **at n=4, reversal pairs all have distance 2 and contribute only 2 mask types. At n=6, reversal pairs span distances {2, 4, 6} and contribute 7 distinct mask types.** The richer reversal structure at n=6 pushes strength up while the algebraic constraint (mask = signature) keeps diversity low but perfectly uniform within its 7 types.

### The "trade-off" is NOT strength vs diversity

Both at n=4 and n=6, strength and diversity are orthogonal. The real KW design choice is:

**High strength + low diversity (algebraically structured) vs. moderate strength + high diversity (random-like).**

KW chooses the former. It doesn't maximize diversity — it maximizes *structured* opposition. The 7 masks it uses are the 7 nonzero elements of the mirror-pair signature group, each used with near-perfect uniformity. This is not maximum entropy — it's maximum algebraic coherence within the strength-diversity space.

### Equivariance is astronomically rare at n=6

At n=4, 117 of 2,027,025 pairings (0.006%) were fully equivariant. At n=6, zero of 10,000 random pairings showed any equivariance under any single group element. Full Z₂²-equivariance at n=6 is essentially a zero-probability event among random pairings. The KW and Shao Yong pairings are both fully equivariant — this alone makes them structurally exceptional.
