# Weakening Analysis: What Each Axiom Contributes

## The complete landscape

187,460 unordered triples of FPF involutions on 8 elements. 10 distinct overlap patterns. 4 patterns produce S₄ (total 50,400 triples, 26.9%).

## What each weakening reveals

### 1. Two involutions can't reach S₄

Maximum group from 2 FPF involutions: **order 8**. The 5,460 pairs generate only three group sizes:
- Order 4 (V₄): 1,260 pairs — all commuting
- Order 6 (S₃): 1,680 pairs — none commuting
- Order 8 (D₄): 2,520 pairs — none commuting

**Three is the exact minimum** for S₄. This is not a design preference — it's a mathematical constraint. You cannot structure 4 blocks with fewer than 3 independent pairings.

### 2. The overlap pattern determines the structural class

| Pattern | Total | S₄ | Purity | Other groups |
|---------|-------|-----|--------|-------------|
| (0,0,0) | 32,970 | 0 | 0% | Z₂², D₄, order-16/32/64, PSL(2,7) |
| **(0,0,1)** | **60,480** | **30,240** | **50%** | **PSL(2,7), order-48** |
| (0,0,2) | 26,460 | 0 | 0% | D₄, order-16/32/64 |
| (0,1,1) | 30,240 | 10,080 | 33% | PSL(2,7) |
| (0,1,2) | 20,160 | 0 | 0% | order-48 only |
| (0,2,2) | 1,260 | 0 | 0% | D₄ only |
| (1,1,1) | 5,600 | 5,040 | 90% | S₃ |
| **(1,1,2)** | **5,040** | **5,040** | **100%** | **— (pure S₄)** |
| (1,2,2) | 5,040 | 0 | 0% | A₄ only |
| (2,2,2) | 210 | 0 | 0% | D₄ only |

**Two paths to pure S₄:**
1. **Overlap (1,1,2) alone** → 100% S₄, no additional axiom needed. This is **Rep B** — every pair of involutions shares at least one pair. Dense overlap, no bridge structure.
2. **Overlap (0,0,1) + commutation** → 100% S₄, zero false positives. This is **Rep A** — one overlap (Kan↔Li), two disjoint pairs, bridge structure. The traditional choice.

### 3. Commutation: not necessary for S₄, but perfect selector for Rep A

Among 60,480 triples with overlap (0,0,1):
- 30,240 generate S₄ (50%)
- 20,160 generate PSL(2,7) (33.3%)
- 10,080 generate order-48 group (16.7%)

Of the 30,240 S₄ triples:
- 20,160 have exactly one commuting zero-overlap pair (Rep A)
- 10,080 have no commuting zero-overlap pair (Rep B realized within (0,0,1))

Adding commutation selects exactly the 20,160 Rep A triples: **100% S₄, zero false positives.** It eliminates all PSL(2,7), all order-48, and the Rep B realizations.

### 4. Fixed points degrade the structure

Adding even one involution with 2 fixed points to a pair of FPF involutions produces groups of order up to 10,000+ (approaching the full symmetric group). The FPF constraint is load-bearing: it keeps the group small and structured.

## The two paths and their consequences

| Property | Rep A (traditional) | Rep B (alternative) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Overlap pattern | (0,0,1) | (1,1,2) |
| Additional axiom needed | Commutation | None |
| Bridge pair | Yes (Kan↔Li) | No — every pair of involutions overlaps |
| Mediator involution | Yes (ι₂ bridges ι₁↔ι₃) | No distinguished mediator |
| V₄ elements | All FPF | Have fixed points |
| 4-cycles | FPF | Have fixed points |
| Structural character | Sparse connections, one bridge | Dense connections, no bridge |

**The tradition chose Rep A: sparse connections with a bridge.** This creates the ι₁-ι₂-ι₃ hierarchy where Lo Shu/KW mediates between Fu Xi and He Tu. Rep B would give a more symmetric structure — every involution equally connected to every other — but no distinguished axis, no mediator, no Kan↔Li hinge.

## What the FPF requirement does

"Why FPF?" = "Why must every trigram have a partner?"

Without FPF (allowing fixed points), the group generated by three involutions can be anything from trivial to nearly S₈. The FPF constraint does two things:
1. **Bounds the group size.** FPF involutions on 8 elements generate groups of order ≤ 168 (PSL(2,7) is the maximum). With fixed points, groups up to order 40,320 (S₈) are possible.
2. **Forces block structure.** A complete pairing of 8 elements is 4 pairs — which becomes 4 blocks. Allowing fixed points means some elements are unpaired, breaking the block decomposition.

FPF is not just an aesthetic choice. It's the constraint that keeps the algebra finite, structured, and block-decomposed.

## The group landscape

Full analysis in `group_properties.py`. 10 groups arise from FPF triples on 8 elements (orders 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 168).

### S₄ vs PSL(2,7): categories vs formless symmetry

PSL(2,7) (order 168) is the largest group FPF triples can generate. It is simple (no normal subgroups), primitive (no block systems), and transitive. It gives maximum symmetry on 8 elements but **zero categorical structure** — no way to group elements into types that the group respects. Any partition gets scrambled.

S₄ (order 24) has exactly **1 block system** of 4×2. It preserves the categorical decomposition: 4 types, 2 flavors each. It has a normal chain S₄ ⊃ A₄ ⊃ V₄ ⊃ {id} — internal structure you can decompose into layers.

PSL(2,7) is a bag of 8 interchangeable atoms. S₄ is a periodic table. You need the table to do chemistry.

### Rep A vs Rep B: depth of complementarity

Both are S₄ on the same block structure. The difference is how deeply the FPF property penetrates beyond the generators.

| | Rep A (traditional) | Rep B |
|---|---|---|
| FPF elements | **15/24 (63%)** | 6/24 (25%) |
| FPF involutions | 9 | 6 |
| 4-cycles | (4,4) — all FPF | (4,2,1,1) — have fixed points |

In Rep A, the "everything has an opposite" principle is **generative** — it propagates from the three axiom involutions through their compositions. 63% of the group moves everything. The complementarity saturates the algebra.

In Rep B, the principle is **skin-deep** — the three generators are FPF by definition, but their compositions mostly leave elements fixed (75% of the group has fixed points). The 4-cycles move 4 elements, swap 2, and leave 2 untouched. Complementarity holds at the axiom level but breaks down under composition.

The tradition chose the representation where the founding principle pervades the structure, not just the surface.
