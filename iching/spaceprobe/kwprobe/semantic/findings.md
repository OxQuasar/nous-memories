# Semantic-Algebraic Bridge: Grain-Dependent Coupling

## The Question

Two independent analyses of the King Wen sequence converged on the same object from opposite directions:

- **Algebraic (kwprobe):** Basin structure, 互 convergence, kernel algebra, inter-pair bridges. Verdict: "the ordering is semantic with algebraic consequences." Best reconstruction: 7/31 (23%).
- **Semantic (iter8):** Xugua reading, corridor structure, two-regime model. Verdict: pair ordering is the joint trace of corridor structure (~29 bits) and causal narrative (~57 bits).

The question: is the semantic-algebraic bridge revealing a deeper unity, confirming genuine independence, or something else?

## The Answer: Structural Complementarity with a Specific Grain

**Neither unity nor independence. The coupling has a grain — the pair.**

| Scale | Coupling | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orientation** (1 bit/pair) | Complete | 32/32 developmental priority = algebraic Pareto optimality, p < 10⁻⁵ | Proven |
| **Individual bridges** (multi-dimensional) | Independent | All 6 cross-tabs null, all p ≥ 0.524 | Proven |
| **Aggregate statistics** (whole-sequence) | Emergent | Basin clustering 0th %ile, chiastic asymmetry p=0.026, attractor framing p~10⁻⁷ | Proven |
| **Preserving bridges** (n=9) | Suggestive | Lower→causal 4/5, upper→cyclical 2/4; p=0.524 | Not confirmable |

### What was tested

Six cross-tabulations at the individual bridge level, in both corridor-rich (n=21) and corridor-free (n=10) zones:

| Cross-tab | Rich zone p | Free zone p |
|---|---|---|
| H-kernel × Logic type (Causal/Cyclical) | 1.000 | 0.524 |
| 互 smoothness × Confidence (Direct/Implied) | 0.659 | 1.000 |
| Basin-crossing × Directionality (→/⇀) | 1.000 | 0.571 |

**No algebraic feature predicts any semantic feature at the individual transition level.** This holds equally in both regimes — the corridor-rich and corridor-free zones show no detectable difference in algebra-semantics coupling.

Additionally: among the 22 non-preserving bridges, 互 smoothness (hidden transition magnitude) shows zero correlation with Xugua confidence. Implied rate among smooth transitions: 29%. Among rough: 33%. Fisher p = 1.000.

## The Three-Level Picture

### Level 1: Orientation (complete coupling)

Within each pair, the choice of which hexagram comes first is simultaneously determined by:
- **Algebraic criterion:** Pareto optimality across four structural axes (S=2-free, trigram balance, developmental priority, Hamming gradient)
- **Semantic criterion:** Developmental priority — the more primary situation precedes

These coincide perfectly. 32/32. One bit per pair is small enough for two independent constraint systems to over-determine simultaneously.

### Level 2: Individual bridges (independence)

Between pairs, each transition carries multiple categorical dimensions — logic type, confidence, directionality, basin-crossing, kernel class, 互 distance, corridor relationship. At this resolution, the algebraic and semantic descriptions see genuinely different things:

- **Algebra sees:** basin topology, kernel symmetry, 互 continuity
- **Semantics sees:** developmental narrative, thematic kinship, exhaustion-reversal rhythms

These do not predict each other. The Xugua's assessment of *why* one pair follows another is independent of the algebraic profile of the transition.

### Level 3: Aggregate (emergent coupling)

At the whole-sequence level, signal re-emerges:
- Basin clustering at 0th percentile
- Chiastic canon asymmetry at p = 0.026 (UC→Kun, LC→Qian)
- Attractor framing at p ≈ 5×10⁻⁷ (fixed points open, limit cycle closes)

These signatures are real but emergent — they arise because semantic categories correlate with binary structure at the population level, not transition-by-transition.

## The Preserving Bridge Note

The 9 preserving bridges (where one trigram persists across the pair boundary) show an interpretable but unconfirmable pattern:

| Position | Causal | Cyclical | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower preserved | 4 | 1 | 80% Causal |
| Upper preserved | 2 | 2 | 50% Cyclical |

Fisher p = 0.524 on n = 9. The sample size is structurally fixed — there will never be more than 9 preserving bridges.

**Interpretive reading:** Lower trigram = foundation, ground, condition. When the ground persists, the transition is causal — the preserved trigram is the *channel* through which cause operates. Upper trigram = consequence, canopy. When the canopy persists, the transition tends toward cyclical — the preserved trigram is *what survives exhaustion*.

The exception (T26: lower Mountain preserved, Cyclical — "stillness cannot last") is itself coherent: Mountain *as ground* is precisely the ground of stillness that gives way.

**Status:** Consistent with the framework, structurally coherent, permanently at n=9. A note, not a finding.

## Why This Pattern

Coupling appears wherever the question compresses to approximately one bit. It vanishes wherever the question expands to multiple categorical dimensions at small N.

This is not a statistical artifact — it reflects the structure of the object:

1. **The pair is the natural unit** at which algebra and meaning converge. Both descriptions resolve a pair's internal structure identically.

2. **Between pairs, they are genuinely different projections** — different angles on the same arrangement, not translations of each other.

3. **At the aggregate level, different projections produce the same statistics** because both are consequences of arranging 32 pairs of situations in a coherent order. Semantic coherence creates algebraic clustering without optimizing for it.

The 8 cyclical ("exhaustion") transitions show no general basin correspondence (crossing rate 63% vs 68% base rate). **However**: the 2 direct Kun↔Qian inter-pair crossings are both cyclical (hypergeometric p = 0.048). All 14 causal crossings route through KanLi. This is the one specific bridge-level correspondence: exhaustion-reversal coincides with the rarest algebraic move (direct fixed-point basin jump). Sample is structurally fixed at n=2.

### The Xugua's position

The Xugua re-derives the ordering logic using a hex-chain grammar (29/31 transitions are hex-to-hex, not pair-to-pair). Its causal chains and cyclical formulas carry no information about the algebraic profile of each transition. It sees the sequence from a genuinely different angle than the algebra — but both describe the same object, and their aggregate shadows coincide.

## What Remains Unexplained

1. **Why these pairs in this order.** The 7/31 ceiling is a wall, not a glass ceiling. The remaining 24/31 bridge choices are determined by something the algebraic framework cannot access. The Xugua provides *reasons* but not a *generator* — it re-derives rather than constructs.

2. **The preserving bridge pattern** (n=9, forever). Interpretively coherent, statistically untestable.

3. **The Kun↔Qian exclusivity** (n=2, forever). A structural fact about this particular sequence, not a generalizable law.

4. **The cross-canon bridge.** Hex 30→31 (kernel = OM) breaks the M-I lock characterizing the Upper Canon. The corridor-rich/free boundary falls nearby. Whether this is structural necessity or a seam between independently composed halves is unresolved.

5. **Selection effects.** Does the specific hexagram content of the Upper Canon inherently support higher algebraic regularity? Untested — the semantic investigation confirms the UC is corridor-rich but cannot determine whether corridor-richness causes algebraic regularity or both are consequences of pair selection.
