# Magic Square Investigation — Open Questions

## Answered

**Q1. Does the 2×3×5 cube carry additive magic properties?**
No. The cube actively resists additive balance. Its native magic is modular (complement equivariance). The polarity axis (prime 2) makes polarity-magic arithmetically impossible. (R1–R5)

**Q2. Does the 6×5 grid carry magic-like balance?**
No. The 納甲 branch cycle over-constrains the grid through the Earth invariant and tripartition. Coverage is highly structured, not balanced. (R6–R15)

**Q3. Does the 5×5 magic square correlate with hexagram properties?**
No. Population-magic correlation is zero. The Siamese construction reduces to 生 × 克, which is the Z₅ stride structure already contained in the algebra. (R16–R21)

**Q4. Do the three faces share a unified notion of "magic"?**
No. Each face has its own: modular (cube), combinatorial (grid), arithmetic (torus). These are incommensurable — they share (3,5) but not a balance condition. The failure modes are prime-indexed. (S1)

**Q5. Is the sum = 15 coincidence between Lo Shu and algebraic trigram sum independent?**
No. It follows from the Mersenne condition 2^{n−1} = n+1 via n²+1 = 2p. Downstream of the uniqueness theorem. (R1, S4)

## Open

**Q6. Why does prime 3 appear three times independently?**
As Fano lines (algebraic), Lo Shu dimension (spatial), and branch cycle factor (calendrical: 12 = 4×3). Each produces different structural consequences aligning at (3,5). The algebraic and spatial appearances follow from well-understood mathematics. The calendrical appearance (why 12 branches? why stride 2?) is a historical/design question. The tripartition depends on the calendrical 3 being load-bearing — if the branch cycle were 10-fold (2×5) instead of 12-fold (4×3), the tripartition would break and the Earth invariant would fail.

**Q7. What determines the Z₅ sum mod 5 class of a hexagram?**
The distribution {≡0: 12, ≡1: 6, ≡2: 24, ≡3: 2, ≡4: 20} is exactly predicted by trigram-level convolution, and the sole source of lower/upper asymmetry is 坤. But what structural property of a hexagram (beyond its trigram pair) correlates with its mod-5 class has not been tested against operational properties (e.g., 用神 selection, divination outcome type).

**Q8. Does the yin/yang → 生/克 diagonal mapping extend beyond 乾 and 坤?**
Finding R10 shows yang branches produce 克-type (equal-sum) pairs and yin produce 生-type (unequal-sum) pairs for the two primordial trigrams. Whether this pattern extends to other trigrams' branch assignments (which are neither pure-yang nor pure-yin) has not been tested. The mixed trigrams might interpolate between the two diagonal types.

**Q9. Is the Earth invariant unique to the traditional 納甲 assignment?**
Every trigram gets exactly 1 Earth — but would alternative stride assignments (e.g., stride 1 or stride 3 through the 12-branch cycle) also produce this? If stride 2 is the only stride that forces the Earth invariant, then the 納甲 system's specific branch assignment rule is load-bearing, not interchangeable.
