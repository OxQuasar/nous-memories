## Executive Summary

### Key findings:

1. **49 distinct element profiles** among 64 hexagrams — determined uniquely by the
   (lower trigram, upper trigram) element pattern pair. One profile (W→W→E→E→W→M) 
   appears 4 times; 12 profiles appear twice; 36 appear once.

2. **⚡ Every hexagram has exactly 2 Earth lines.** This is a structural invariant of
   納甲: every trigram contributes exactly 1 Earth to its 3-line element assignment.
   Earth column sum = 128 globally, = 16 per palace — a universal constant.

3. **Grid coverage is highly structured, not uniform.** Expected 12.8 per cell;
   actual range [0, 24]. Two cells are never marked (L1-Metal, L2-Metal).
   Variance = 58.03, far above uniform.

4. **Element columns are very unequal:** Earth=128, Wood=80, Fire=64, Water=64, Metal=48.
   Earth dominates at 2× its fair share (128 vs expected 76.8).

5. **59 distinct 六親 profiles** (nearly all unique — 54 singletons, 5 doublets).
   All 5 六親 types present in 16/64 hexagrams. All 5 elements present in 16/64 hexagrams.
   These are the SAME 16 hexagrams — forced by the Z₅ cycle: having all 5 elements
   guarantees all 5 (L−P) mod 5 residues, hence all 5 六親 types. A tautology.

6. **六親 line position preferences:** L1 is strongly dominated by 父母 (29/64 = 45%);
   L2 by 官鬼 (28/64 = 44%); L3 by 兄弟 (26/64 = 41%). Upper lines (L4-L6) are more balanced.

7. **⚡ Per-palace max 六親 is always exactly 16, explained by Earth invariant.**
   Every palace's most frequent 六親 = the relation Earth has to the palace element:
   Wood→妻財(16), Fire→子孫(16), Earth→兄弟(16), Metal→父母(16), Water→官鬼(16).
   Verified 8/8 palaces. The structural chain: 納甲 gives every trigram exactly 1 Earth
   → every hexagram has exactly 2 Earth → 16 Earth per palace → the 六親 of Earth
   relative to the palace element is always the dominant relation with count ≥ 16.

8. **⚡ Z₅ element sums are always multiples of 3.** Every trigram's 3-element Z₅ sum ≡ 0 mod 3.
   Since hexagram sum = lower_sum + upper_sum, hexagram sums are ≡ 0 mod 3.
   The 5 observed values {6, 9, 12, 15, 18} = {2×3, 3×3, 4×3, 5×3, 6×3}.
   Trigram sums take only 3 values: {3, 6, 9}.
   The sum mod 5 distribution {0:12, 1:6, 2:24, 3:2, 4:20} is highly non-uniform.
