# Resolution Investigation — Questions

> If the I Ching is a minimal Markov partition — the smallest projection that captures binary exclusion, separated timescales, and inversion symmetry — what is it projecting?

## R1: Logistic Map Identification

Does the I Ching's 64-state transition structure match the 2-step symbolic dynamics of the logistic map at the Fibonacci parameter (onset of GMS constraint)?

The 克 subgraph on Q₃ is the bipartite double cover of the GMS (R270). The GMS arises as the symbolic dynamics of unimodal maps at a specific parameter. If the full 64-state structure (not just 克) matches the logistic map's partition at that parameter, the projection target is identified.

## R2: Timescale Ratio Matching

Do the spectral ratios {1, √2, φ} appear as characteristic timescale ratios in any known multi-process system?

Three Chebyshev-separated timescales. Survey: ecological (disturbance/recovery/succession), physiological (neural/hormonal/developmental), economic (transaction/business cycle/structural), climate (weather/seasonal/glacial). Measure timescale ratios and check for {1, √2, φ} spacing.

## R3: Regime Transition Statistics

Does the GMS forbidden-pattern constraint (no consecutive "high" states in the destructive channel) hold in empirical regime-change data?

Formalize the traditional claim: 比和 = continuity, 生 = generation, 克 = destruction. Classify observed transitions in political, ecological, or economic regime data. Check whether consecutive 克 states are forbidden or suppressed. Check whether 克→生 transitions are absent (the valve, R262).

## R4: Effective Dimensionality

Does the ~16-dim semantic manifold correspond to the effective dimension of any candidate target system?

The text spans ~16 independent dimensions (weight-3 Walsh eigenspace, R254). If this is the effective dimension of the thing being projected, it constrains the target. Systems with ~16 effective DOF: climate (10-20 EOFs), gene networks (10-20 master regulators), macro-economics (10-20 key variables).

## R5: Dark Sector Resolution

What do the 60 uncharacterized hex-level eigenvalues control, and how do they degrade the projection's resolution at long timescales?

The coherent sector (4 eigenvalues from trigram level) is subdominant: ρ_coherent = 2φ ≈ 3.24 < ρ_total = 4.69. The dark sector dominates long-time dynamics. Until characterized, we don't know what the projection loses over time.

## R6: Composability and Sequential Resolution

Does the projection preserve enough information for multi-step reasoning, or do errors compound?

This connects to Q2 (composability, the sole remaining open structural question from reversal). If hexagrams compose meaningfully — if the transition from hex A to hex B to hex C preserves information about the A→C trajectory — then the projection supports sequential prediction. If not, the resolution is single-step only.
