# 火珠林 Opening Example: Gravesite Divination

From §易中明義. The text's first worked example, demonstrating all three
layers of the system (天干=heaven, 地支=human affairs, 納音=earth/geography).

---

## The Question

Someone asks about a burial site. The coins are cast and produce
**姤之鼎** — hexagram 姤 (Encounter) with a moving line that transforms
it into 鼎 (The Cauldron).

## The Hexagram

**姤 (天風姤)** — Heaven above, Wind below. 乾宮 一世卦.
Palace element: **金 Metal**.

```
         干支    五行  六親    位
上六    壬戌    土    父母
九五    壬申○   金    兄弟          ← 動爻 (moving line)
九四    壬午    火    官鬼    應
九三    辛酉    金    兄弟
九二    辛亥    水    子孫
初六    辛丑    土    父母    世
```

Line 5 (壬申金) is the moving line. It transforms into 己未火,
producing the changed hexagram 鼎 (離上巽下).

### 飛伏 (Visible and Hidden)

姤 is the first derived hexagram from pure 乾. Its hidden (伏) lines
come from the palace root 乾:

| Line | 飛 (visible) | 伏 (hidden) |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| 初   | 辛丑 土 父母 | **甲子 水** |
| 二   | 辛亥 水 子孫 | 甲寅 木 |
| 三   | 辛酉 金 兄弟 | 甲辰 土 |
| 四   | 壬午 火 官鬼 | (same) |
| 五   | 壬申 金 兄弟 | (same) |
| 上   | 壬戌 土 父母 | (same) |

Critical: the 世 line (初六 辛丑土) has **甲子水** hidden beneath it.
甲子's 納音 is **海中金** — gold buried in the sea. Metal hidden under Earth.

### 六親 Assignment

Palace = 金 Metal:
- 土 generates 金 → **父母** (lines 初, 上)
- 水 is generated by 金 → **子孫** (line 二)
- 金 = same → **兄弟** (lines 三, 五)
- 火 overcomes 金 → **官鬼** (line 四)
- 木 is overcome by 金 → **妻財** (not present in visible lines)

Note: 妻財 (Wood) is entirely absent from the hexagram. It exists
only in the hidden line 甲寅木 under line 2.

## The Reading

The text delivers seven predictions about the gravesite, each derived
from a different mechanism:

### 1. 「土中有石」 — Stone underground

> 世持辛丑，土伏甲子金，世下伏金，是土中有石也。

**Mechanism: 飛伏 (visible/hidden)**

The 世 line is 辛丑 **土**. Hidden beneath it is 甲子, whose 納音 is
海中**金**. Metal concealed under Earth = stone buried in soil.

The reading moves from the abstract (elemental relationship) to the
concrete (stone) through the 納音's imagery: 海中金 evokes something
hard and metallic enclosed within a medium.

### 2. 「其色大赤」 — The stone is deep red

> 巽下伏乾，是乾為大赤也。

**Mechanism: 八卦 correspondence (trigram imagery)**

姤's lower trigram is 巽. Its hidden counterpart (from the palace root
乾) is 乾 itself. 乾 = 大赤 (great red) in the trigram color system.

The hidden trigram beneath the surface (巽) is pure Heaven (乾),
which gives the color of what's concealed.

### 3. 「伏屍鬼」 — A buried corpse

> 第五爻壬申化己未火，火克本宮為鬼，是伏屍鬼。

**Mechanism: 動爻 transformation + 六親**

Line 5 (壬申金) moves and transforms into 己未火.
Fire overcomes the palace element Metal → 官鬼 (ghost/authority).
A 鬼 produced by transformation = a spirit that has changed state = a corpse.

### 4. 「西南方」 — The corpse is to the southwest

> 申化未是西南方也。

**Mechanism: 地支 directional mapping**

壬**申** transforms into 己**未**. 未 = southwest in the twelve-branch
compass. The direction of the transformation points to the corpse's location.

### 5. 「掘下五尺見石」 — Dig five feet to find the stone

> 土類五也。

**Mechanism: 五行 numerical correspondence**

The 世 line is 土 (Earth). Earth's number is **五** (5).
Depth = the element's number = five feet.

### 6. 「離穴四十步有伏屍」 — Forty paces from the grave to the corpse

> 壬申金，重數四，加丑未土類五，二五成十，並申金四是四十步也。

**Mechanism: 納音 arithmetic**

This is the most complex derivation:

1. 壬**申** — 申 = Metal's number = **4**
2. 丑 and 未 (from 辛丑 世 and 己未 變) — both are 土, Earth's number = **5**
3. Two 土 values: 5 × 2 = **10**
4. Total: 10 × 4(申) = **40** paces

The distance is computed by multiplying the element-numbers of the
branches involved in the transformation chain.

### 7. 「出刀傷之人」 — Death by blade

> 壬申乃劍鋒金也。

**Mechanism: 納音 imagery**

壬申's 納音 is **劍鋒金** — "Sword-edge Metal." The moving line's
納音 gives the cause of death. 劍鋒 = a sharp blade. The corpse
died by a cutting weapon.

### 8. 「主火災」 — Fire disaster to come

> 已未化火，未克辛丑世也。

**Mechanism: 五行 生克 on the 世 line**

己未 is 天上**火** (by 納音). 未 also 克 辛丑 (the 世 line) through
the branch relationship. Fire attacking the self = fire disaster
threatening the querent.

### 9. 「近柳樹」 — Near a willow tree

> 己未火鬼，與壬午木合住，壬午乃楊柳木也。

**Mechanism: 六合 (branch combination) + 納音 imagery**

己未 (the transformed fire-ghost) combines (合) with 壬午.
壬午's 納音 is **楊柳木** — "Willow Wood." The ghost is bound
to a willow tree through the six-harmony branch pairing (午未合).

## The Architecture

The example demonstrates the three-layer system announced at the
opening of the chapter:

| Layer | Governs | Example in this reading |
|-------|---------|------------------------|
| **天干** (stems) | Heaven, celestial | 壬/辛 stems identify the trigram origins |
| **地支** (branches) | Human affairs | 申→未 gives direction (southwest), 丑未 give depth |
| **納音** (resonance) | Earth, material | 海中金=stone, 劍鋒金=blade, 楊柳木=willow |

Each prediction uses a different combination of mechanisms:

| Prediction | Mechanisms used |
|------------|-----------------|
| Stone underground | 飛伏 + 納音 |
| Red color | 八卦 trigram correspondence |
| Buried corpse | 動爻 + 六親 |
| Southwest direction | 地支 compass |
| Five feet deep | 五行 number |
| Forty paces | 納音 arithmetic |
| Blade death | 納音 imagery |
| Fire disaster | 五行 生克 on 世 |
| Willow tree | 六合 + 納音 imagery |

The single hexagram is read through nine different lenses simultaneously.
No single mechanism dominates — the practitioner moves fluidly between
層 (layers), selecting whichever mechanism produces the most specific
answer to each aspect of the question.

## Second Example: Ghost Identification (遁之姤)

The text immediately follows with a second example using a different
question type — identifying a haunting spirit.

**遁 (天山遁)** transforming to **姤 (天風姤)**. 乾宮 三世卦.

```
         干支    五行  六親    位
上九    壬戌    土    父母    應
九五    壬申    金    兄弟
九四    壬午    火    官鬼
九三    丙申    金    兄弟    世
六二    丙午○   火    官鬼          ← 動爻
初六    丙辰    土    父母
```

Line 2 (丙午火 官鬼) moves, transforming into 辛亥水 (子孫).

### The Reading

**「子孫鬼」— A descendant-ghost (ghost of a child/young person)**

丙午火 is 官鬼. It transforms into 辛亥水 which is 子孫.
A ghost (鬼) that transforms into offspring (子孫) = a ghost
whose nature is that of a young dependent.

丙午's 納音 is 天河**水**. The branch 亥 is also 水.
Both original and transformed share Water nature → confirms
the 子孫 connection (Water is 子孫 in 乾宮: 金生水).

**「一男一女」— One male and one female**

丙午 = 陽 (yang stem 丙, yang branch 午) → male.
辛亥 = 陰 (yin stem 辛, yin branch 亥) → female.

**「男兒赤，性燥」— The male is red-faced and hot-tempered**

丙午 = 火. Fire's color is red. Fire's nature is volatile/dry.

**「女兒潔白，性剛」— The female is pale and rigid**

辛亥's 納音 is 釵釧**金**. Metal's color is white. Metal's nature is hard/firm.
釵釧 = hairpin and bracelet — ornamental metal objects on a woman.

**「墳墓現在西北」— The grave is in the northwest**

Water's 墓 (tomb position) is at 戌. Fire's 絕 (extinction position) is at 亥.
亥 = the northwest direction. Both tomb-indicators converge on the northwest.

**「恐有動犯」— The grave has been disturbed**

The lower trigram 艮 (Mountain, 土) transforms into 巽 (Wind, 木).
Wood overcomes Earth → 木克土 → the mountain/grave-mound is being
attacked. Something has disturbed the earth of the burial site.

## Structural Observations

### The method is combinatorial, not reductive

Unlike the 梅花 approach (which projects 6 bits → 4 → 2, reading 五行
at each depth), 火珠林 keeps all six lines active and reads across
multiple interpretive systems simultaneously:

- **Positional** (which line is 世/應, inner/outer trigram)
- **Relational** (六親 from palace element)
- **Temporal** (旺衰 from season)
- **Transformational** (動爻 → 變爻, before/after comparison)
- **Hidden** (飛伏, what lurks beneath)
- **Resonant** (納音, the material/imagistic layer)
- **Numerical** (五行 numbers, branch arithmetic)
- **Directional** (地支 compass, 八卦 orientation)

Each system answers a different *kind* of question about the same situation.
The art lies in knowing which system to invoke for which aspect of the query.

### 納音 as the material layer

The most striking feature of these examples is the 納音 system —
the 60-甲子 cycle of poetic metal/water/fire/wood/earth names
(海中金, 劍鋒金, 楊柳木, 天上火, 釵釧金...). This is the layer
that produces the most concrete, imagistic predictions:

- Not just "metal" but *sword-edge* metal → a blade
- Not just "wood" but *willow* wood → a specific tree
- Not just "metal" but *hairpin-bracelet* metal → a woman's ornaments

The 納音 is where the abstract 五行 system acquires texture and specificity.
It is the bridge between algebraic structure and lived reality — the point
where mathematics becomes imagery.
