## E1: Timescale Ratio Matching

Do the spectral ratios {1, √2, φ} appear as characteristic timescale ratios in multi-process systems?

Three Chebyshev-separated timescales from the path graph decomposition (P₂, P₃, P₄). If a natural system has three dominant processes whose relaxation times or oscillation frequencies have ratios √2 and φ, it lives in the constraint class.

Candidates:
- Ecological: disturbance/recovery/succession cycles
- Neural oscillation bands: gamma/beta/alpha frequency ratios
- Predator-prey-resource: fast predation / medium reproduction / slow resource renewal
- Market microstructure: tick/daily/structural timescales
- Climate: weather/seasonal/glacial

## E2: Regime Transition Statistics

Does the GMS forbidden-pattern constraint hold in empirical regime-change data?

The prediction: in systems with binary exclusion and inversion symmetry, consecutive destructive states are suppressed and destruction never directly produces generation.

Steps:
1. Find datasets with classified regime transitions (political science, ecology, finance)
2. Map regimes to 比和/生/克 categories (continuity/generation/destruction)
3. Count transition type bigrams
4. Test: is 克-克 suppressed? Is 克→生 absent or suppressed?

Best candidate datasets:
- Polity IV (political regime changes)
- Ecological regime shift database
- Market regime classification (bull/bear/sideways)

**Key difficulty:** the mapping from empirical categories to 比和/生/克 is not given. Multiple mappings should be tested. Confirmation bias risk is high.

## E4: Inversion Symmetry in Natural Transitions

Do natural systems with regime transitions exhibit complement-like symmetry?

The complement involution (all bits flip) is the I Ching's most structurally central feature. In an external system, this would mean: every state has an "opposite" state, and the transition rules respect the pairing. Examples:
- Bull/bear symmetry in markets
- Excitatory/inhibitory symmetry in neural networks
- Predator/prey role inversion in ecology

If a natural system has approximate complement symmetry AND binary exclusion, it's in the constraint class.
