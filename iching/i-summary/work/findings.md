# Computation Results: Open Questions Investigation

## A: NQ4 — Hamming Syndrome Structure at (4,13) [PRIMARY]

### Setup

At (n=4, p=13): 8 complement pairs in F₂⁴, Frame = {0,15}. The 7 non-Frame pairs map to the 7 nonzero vectors of F₂³ via the quotient F₂⁴/⟨1111⟩. These 7 points form PG(2,F₂) = the Fano plane.

The kernel of the pair-fixing action is (Z₂)³ (order 8), generated by maps g(x) = x ⊕ λ(x)·1111 where λ is a linear functional with λ(1111) = 0.

Orientation vector v ∈ (Z₂)⁷: for each non-Frame pair j, v_j = 0 if f(rep_j) ≤ 6, else 1.

Hamming parity-check matrix H (3×7): columns = binary representations of 1,...,7 = the 7 nonzero vectors of F₂³. This is the standard [7,4,3] Hamming code parity-check.

Syndrome s = Hv mod 2 ∈ F₂³ classifies v into 8 cosets of the Hamming code.

### Key Results

**The 960 orbits do NOT decompose as 8 × 120.**

| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Total surjections (one type dist) | 92,160 |
| Total orbits (Kernel × Aut(Z₁₃)) | 960, all size 96 |
| Syndromes per orbit | **6** (not 1, not 8) |
| Distinct orientation vectors | 64 of 128 (constrained by Type-0 pair) |
| Orientation vectors per syndrome class | 8 |
| Syndrome distribution over all surjections | **Perfectly uniform**: 11,520 per syndrome (12.5% each) |

**The missing-syndrome structure is perfectly symmetric:**

| Missing Z₂ pair | Orbits |
|------------------|--------|
| {0, 1} | 240 |
| {2, 3} | 240 |
| {4, 5} | 240 |
| {6, 7} | 240 |
| **Total** | **960** |

Each orbit visits exactly 6 syndromes, missing one Z₂ pair. The 4 Z₂ pairs correspond to cosets of ⟨(1,0,0)⟩ in F₂³.

### Mechanism: Why 6, not 1 or 8?

**The kernel flip patterns are Hamming codewords** (all 8 raw flip vectors have syndrome 0). In the full F₂⁷ theory, this would mean kernels preserve syndromes → 1 syndrome per orbit.

**The Type-0 pair breaks this.** Pair 1 has type 0 (both reps → 0), so v[0] = 0 always. When a kernel element with λ(1) = 1 acts, the raw flip has flip[0] = 1, but the actual orientation doesn't flip (because f(14) = 0 still). The *effective* flip differs from the raw flip at position 0: it's the raw flip with position 0 forced to 0.

**Result:** The effective flip vectors have syndrome ∈ {0, 1}. Half the kernel (λ(1) = 0) preserves syndromes; half (λ(1) = 1) shifts by ⊕1. This creates the Z₂ pairing: {0,1}, {2,3}, {4,5}, {6,7}.

**Negation (α = 12)** flips all non-Type-0 positions → flip pattern (0,1,1,1,1,1,1) → syndrome shift ⊕1. Same shift as kernel, so negation doesn't reach new cosets.

**Higher Aut(Z₁₃) elements** (α = 2, 3, ...) do NOT simply flip orientation vectors — they permute values within negation pairs in complex ways. The net effect: each orbit visits exactly 3 of the 4 Z₂ cosets, missing one.

### Within-orbit syndrome distributions

Two sub-types exist within each missing-pair class:

| Sub-type | Count per class | Syndrome distribution |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Uniform | 96 orbits | 16 surjections per syndrome |
| Biased | 144 orbits | (8, 8, 8, 8, 32, 32) per syndrome |
| **Total** | **240 orbits** | |

### Interpretation

The (4,13) object has genuine entanglement between orientation and assignment degrees of freedom. Two mechanisms conspire:

1. **Type-0 desynchronization:** The kernel flips are Hamming codewords in F₂⁷ (syndrome 0), but the Type-0 constraint removes one bit's flip freedom. The *effective* flips have syndrome ∈ {0, 1}, creating a Z₂ pairing on the 8 syndrome classes.

2. **Aut(Z₁₃) coset hopping:** The non-negation automorphisms (α = 2, 3, 4, ...) permute values within negation pairs in assignment-dependent ways. Each orbit's Aut(Z₁₃) action visits exactly 3 of the 4 Z₂ cosets, leaving one missing.

The correct factorization is:

**960 = 4 (missing-coset class) × 240 (orbits per class)**

not 8 × 120. The "8 orientations" structure collapses to a 4-fold classification because the Type-0 constraint breaks the full Hamming symmetry.

Within each class of 240, there are two sub-types:
- 96 orbits with **uniform** syndrome counts (16 each across 6 syndromes)
- 144 orbits with **biased** counts (8,8,8,8,32,32) — two syndromes 4× overrepresented

**Surprise:** The clean product structure fails not due to the Z₃ cube-root action (which was the open question), but due to the Type-0 constraint interacting with the Hamming parity check. The kernel doesn't preserve syndromes — it pairs them. The Z₃ action then selects which 3 of 4 cosets are visited.

**Q3: Does the 96/144 split correlate with the shared negation pair?**
**No.** All 6 negation pairs appear with similar frequency in both uniform and biased orbits. The frame pair values show no clean separation — both orbit types use all 6 frame value pairs. The uniform/biased distinction is not determined by the shared negation pair alone.

**Q4: Kernel vs Aut decomposition within orbits.**
- **Kernel** always generates syndrome orbits of size 2: {s, s⊕1}

- In biased orbits: Aut concentrates 4/12 images at each of the 2 overrepresented syndromes (vs 1/12 at each of the 4 underrepresented)
- In uniform orbits: Aut distributes 2/12 images at each syndrome — perfectly even

**Interpretation:** "Why 凶?" cannot be answered by prevalence-dependent detection power. At 13.5%, both core and shell effects are significantly underpowered (core 51%, shell 44%, dual only 22%). The fact that 凶 is the only dual-coupled marker despite having the lowest prevalence among detected markers makes it MORE surprising, not less. The answer to "why 凶?" must be semantic/textual, not statistical.

## C: C1-C2 — Nuclear Rank Sequence

### Conjecture: rank(M^k) = max(2, 2n − 2k)

**Verified for n = 3, 4, 5, 6.**

| n | 2n | Rank sequence | |Stable| | Status |
|---|-----|---------------|---------|--------|
| 3 | 6 | 4 → 2 → 2 → 2 → 2 | 4 | ✓ |
| 4 | 8 | 6 → 4 → 2 → 2 → 2 → 2 | 4 | ✓ |
| 5 | 10 | 8 → 6 → 4 → 2 → 2 → 2 → 2 | 4 | ✓ |
| 6 | 12 | 10 → 8 → 6 → 4 → 2 → 2 → 2 → 2 | 4 | ✓ |

### Key structural features

1. **Rank drops by exactly 2 per iteration** until reaching 2, then stabilizes. Stabilization occurs at step k = n−1.

2. **Stable image is always |F₂²| = 4 elements**, forming a 2-dimensional subspace. The generators follow a pattern:
   - n=3: 0b10101 and 0b101010 (alternating bits)
   - n=4: 0b1010101 and 0b10101010
   - n=5: 0b101010101 and 0b1010101010
   - n=6: 0b10101010101 and 0b101010101010

3. **The stable image is always {00...0|00...0, 10...1|01...0, 01...0|10...1, 11...1|11...1}** — the 4 elements corresponding to the innermost shear attractor. These are the "alternating" patterns with lower/upper exchange symmetry.

4. **The 2-per-step rank drop is universal**: the nuclear shear peels off exactly one pair of outer coordinates per iteration, regardless of n. This confirms the "shift + shear" factored structure:
   - Position: shift o → m₁ → ··· → m_{n-2} → i → i⊕ī
   - Orbit: shift ō → m̄₁ → ··· → m̄_{n-2} → ī → ī (projection)

### PROVEN for all n ≥ 2.

**Proof sketch** (full proof in `proof_nuclear_rank.md`):

1. **Factored basis:** M = [S E; E S] where S = superdiagonal shift, E = e_{n-1}·e_{n-1}^T.
2. **Block triangularization:** Change of basis σ = p ⊕ q yields M' = [T E; 0 T], T = S + E.
3. **rank(T^k) = max(1, n−k):** T has fixed point 𝟏 preventing nilpotency.
4. **Key lemma:** Φ_k · ker(T^k) = {0} — the rank-1 gate E filters only the innermost component, but ker(T^k) is supported on outer levels.
5. **Conclusion:** ker(M'^k) = ker(T^k) × ker(T^k), so rank = 2·rank(T^k) = max(2, 2n−2k).

Verified computationally at n = 2, ..., 10 (up to 1,048,576 hexagram-analogs).

## D: Q5 — 彖傳 Anomaly Detection

### Prediction test

**Context:** Kun basin (mostly yin, 33.3% yang) has highest 剛/柔 ratio (2.14) — established fact. **Prediction:** Qian basin (mostly yang, 66.7% yang) should have the LOWEST ratio.

### Results

| Basin | n_hex | 剛 | 柔 | 剛/柔 ratio | Yang % |
|-------|-------|-----|-----|------------|--------|
| Kun | 16 | 15 | 7 | **2.14** | 33.3% |
| Cycle | 32 | 30 | 20 | 1.50 | 50.0% |
| Qian | 16 | 15 | 12 | **1.25** | 66.7% |
| **Total** | **64** | **60** | **39** | **1.54** | — |

### Key Finding

**✓ PERFECT MONOTONIC: Kun (2.14) > Cycle (1.50) > Qian (1.25)**

The prediction is confirmed. The 彖傳 systematically comments on what's **unusual** in each basin:

- **Kun basin** (mostly yin hexagrams): 剛/柔 = 2.14 — comments heavily on yang (the rare element)
- **Cycle basin** (balanced): 剛/柔 = 1.50 — balanced, slightly above parity since 剛 is globally more common
- **Qian basin** (mostly yang hexagrams): 剛/柔 = 1.25 — relatively more 柔 commentary (commenting on the rare yin)

The ordering is exactly inverse to the yang-line percentage, as predicted by the "anomaly detector" hypothesis.

### Interpretation

This extends the known result (R93: Kun has highest ratio) to its natural completion: the entire 剛/柔 usage gradient tracks inversely with basin yang-content. The 彖傳 authors preferentially used 剛 when discussing yin-heavy hexagrams and 柔 when discussing yang-heavy ones. This is consistent with a commentary tradition that highlights **structural exceptions** — what deviates from the hexagram's dominant character.

The raw numbers are small (60 剛, 39 柔 across 64 hexagrams), so this is a pattern observation rather than a statistical proof. But the monotonic ordering across 3 non-overlapping basins is noteworthy.
