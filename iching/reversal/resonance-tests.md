# Resonance Tests

The research proved: given binary substrate + two independent cycles, (3,5) is the unique rigid point. The astronomical, seasonal, and lunar cycles interface cleanly with this structure through the same primes {2, 3, 5}. Is this resonance necessary (the algebra captures something about change itself) or contingent (a human construction with beautiful internal structure)?

Three tests, each at a different level.

## Test 1: Alternative Axiom Systems (computational) — COMPLETE

**Status:** Completed. 21 new results (R73–R93). See `Q2/findings.md` for full analysis.

**Answer:** (3,5) is special **only within** the complement-respecting binary framework. But the complement axiom's algebraic form (negation equivariance) is the **unique possibility** within the framing — not one choice among many.

**The three-layer architecture:**
1. **Negation is forced** — the only Aut(Z_p) scaling that admits equivariant surjections in the singleton-forcing regime (p > 2^{n−1}). Theorem.
2. **Axis is a (2ⁿ−1)-fold GL-equivalent choice** — the complement axiom cross-sections a GL-invariant 1.33% subset. 1/7 fraction is group-theoretically forced. Theorem.
3. **Rigidity at (3,5)** — unique rigid point of the orbit formula. Prior theorem.

**Key findings:**
- Without complement equivariance, (3,5) has the MOST orbits (245) among n=3 cases — the least rigid point
- Equivariant surjections form a thin GL-invariant natural subset (5 of 245 orbits)
- The singleton-forcing boundary (p = 2^{n−1}) is a phase transition: below it, richer local symmetry but no rigidity; above it, thinner symmetry but global uniqueness possible
- The structure is necessary given the framing; the framing is coherent but not itself necessary

**Test 1 verdict for the resonance question:** The axioms (binary substrate + polarity + completeness) do the work. Given these axioms, everything is forced. Whether the axioms themselves are the right formalization of "situated change" remains open — that's Q2 proper.

## Test 2: Cross-Cultural Convergence (research) — COMPLETE

**Status:** Completed. 12 new results (R94–R105). See `Q2T2/findings.md` for full analysis.

**Answer:** T2 returns **qualified negative**. Binary encoding converges cross-culturally (≥3 traditions). Five-fold classification partially converges (China, India — same count, different algebra). The dual-cycle surjection structure is uniquely Chinese.

**Key findings:**
- Ifá (Yoruba, West Africa) independently discovered F₂⁴ with complement pairing — the same binary structural insight as the I Ching. But no pentadic codomain, no dual cycles.
- Indian Mahābhūta has 5 elements but as a chain poset P₅ (total order, zero cycles), not cyclic Z₅. Same cardinality, incompatible algebra.
- Japan's Gogyo/Godai contrast is the sharpest evidence: same culture absorbed both Chinese 五行 (with 生/克 cycles) and Indian Mahābhūta (static) — keeping them structurally distinct.
- The Ifá counterfactual: if F₂⁴ had the complement axiom toward Z₅, it would produce 168 orbits (vs 5 at (3,5)). Rigidity requires n=3 specifically.

**T2 verdict for the resonance question:** The framing (binary + pentadic + dual-cycle + surjection) was invented once, in China. The individual components (binary encoding, five-fold classification) partially converge, explaining why the framing is coherent. But assembling them into a complement-respecting surjection is the creative act — done once, algebraically completed once.

## Test 3: Empirical Asymmetry Predictions (testable)

**Status:** Not started. Empirical question.

**Question:** Does the {4,2,2,2,2} cascade — Earth at 4/12 branch positions, Fire/Metal at 2/12 each — predict observable asymmetries in natural 12-fold cycles?

The cascade is not a design choice. It's a consequence of embedding 5 phases into a 12-position cycle. If the five-phase model captures something real about seasonal/cyclical dynamics, and the 12-fold cycle captures something real about temporal periodicity, then the asymmetry should be empirically detectable.

### Predictions

**P1: Earth-phase ubiquity.** Earth-associated phenomena (transition, stability, center, soil/harvest processes) should appear in more months than phenomena associated with any single other phase. Specifically, Earth should be present in 4/12 months vs 2/12 for Fire or Metal.

**P2: Fire/Metal scarcity.** Fire-associated phenomena (heat, expansion, rapid growth) and Metal-associated phenomena (contraction, harvest completion, dryness) should be the most temporally concentrated — appearing in fewer months than Wood or Water phenomena.

**P3: Anti-resonance pattern.** In months where the dominant seasonal phase generates the subordinate phase (a 生 pair), processes associated with both should co-occur. In months where they conflict (a 克 pair), one should suppress the other. The 10/320 anti-resonance states (fc = 0) predict specific month-phase combinations where a complete absence of activity in certain domains is structurally expected.

### Data sources
- Agricultural calendars (traditional Chinese, but also cross-cultural: when do planting, growth, harvest, dormancy actually occur?)
- Ecological cycle data (phenology: flowering times, migration, hibernation across 12 months)
- Meteorological pattern data (temperature, precipitation, day-length as functions of month)
- Economic cycle data (seasonal patterns in production, trade, consumption)

### Method
- Classify monthly phenomena by five-phase association (using traditional Chinese correspondences as the initial mapping, then testing robustness to reclassification)
- Measure the empirical distribution across 12 months
- Compare to {4,2,2,2,2} prediction
- Test robustness: does the asymmetry survive if you use 4 seasons instead of 5? If you use a different element-month mapping?

### What constitutes evidence
- Empirical distribution matching {4,2,2,2,2} across multiple independent datasets → the embedding captures real structure
- Empirical distribution matching but only under the traditional Chinese month-element mapping → the model works but the mapping is doing the work
- No match → the asymmetry is in the model, not in nature
