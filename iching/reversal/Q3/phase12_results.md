## Key Findings

### The Five Judgment Operations

The practitioner's judgment at the 80/20 boundary consists of exactly five recurring operations:

1. **Analogy** (most frequent): Mapping trigram象 to concrete situation. This is the PRIMARY judgment operation — it bridges the abstract五行 structure to the specific question.
2. **Weighting**: Counting and balancing生克 signals. When multiple卦 give conflicting signals, the practitioner must decide which dominates.
3. **Integration**: Combining爻辭 text with卦理 analysis. Neither source alone determines the judgment — both must converge.
4. **External**: Incorporating context the hexagram doesn't contain — time of day, season, the querent's state, the practitioner's own motion.
5. **Exception**: Overriding standard rules. The rarest move, reserved for cases where context contradicts the normal reading (e.g., 西林寺: 比和 should be吉 but context inverts it).

### The Analogy-Dominance Pattern

Analogy appears in 14/16 examples and accounts for 25/63 (40%) of all moves. This is the irreducibly non-algorithmic operation — no formula determines which of a trigram's dozens of象 associations to activate. The practitioner navigates a high-dimensional象 space guided by context.

### The 推數又須明理 Principle

MH03 (axe vs hoe) contains the explicit methodological statement: '推數又須明理' — calculation must be supplemented by reasoning. The external/context move is not an add-on but is doctrinally required. The system is DESIGNED to have an algorithmic gap that judgment fills.

### Connection to Q1 Findings

The 18-dimensional complement opposition (R133) is invisible at the vocabulary level (R135). Two complement hexagrams use different analogies from the same trigram象 space, creating semantic opposition without vocabulary contrast.
