## Six Findings

### 1. Meaning and KW ordering are decisively correlated
32/32. Null rejected at every tier. The orientation bits encode developmental priority: condition → consequence.

### 2. The semantic principle: developmental priority
Not "active first." Not "receptive first." Whatever creates the conditions for the other comes first — whether that condition is active (Revolution), passive (Obstruction), normative (Family), or suffering (Splitting Apart). This operates across multiple registers (peak→decline, problem→resolution, discipline→fruit, spark→endurance, etc.).

### 3. All 4 M-rule exceptions are semantically justified
Where M-component and developmental priority conflict, KW follows meaning. The algebraic hierarchy (kac > M-score) independently produces the same resolution. Two languages, one verdict at every conflict point.

### 4. Algebraic and meaning constraints are complementary
The Round 4 discovery. Where algebra is most rigid (KW-dominates), meaning is weaker (46% Suggestive). Where algebra allows trade-offs, meaning is stronger (26% Suggestive). They cover different pairs. Together they anchor all 32 bits with no residual.

### 5. Meaning disambiguates where algebra cannot
At pair 18 (Jin/Ming Yi), algebra sees a Pareto trade-off. Meaning unambiguously favors KW. At 16 non-M-decisive pairs, algebra is silent. Meaning speaks at all 16.

### 6. The meaning-constraint spectrum
21 pairs overdetermined (reversal clearly inferior). 11 pairs preferred (reversal defensible). 0 pairs indifferent (no pair where reversal is equally good).

## The Complementary Coverage Pattern

| Fragility class | Clear | Suggestive | Sugg rate |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| KW-dominates (algebra rigid) | 7 | 6 | 46% |
| Trade-off (algebra flexible) | 14 | 5 | 26% |

This inverse correlation rules out three simpler stories:
1. **Meaning generates algebra** → they'd be strong at the same places. They aren't.
2. **Algebra generates meaning** → meaning would be uniformly strong/weak. It isn't.
3. **Independence** → coverage would be random, not complementary. It isn't.

What remains: **two descriptions of one thing, each capturing what the other misses.**
