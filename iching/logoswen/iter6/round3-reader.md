## UPPER CANON — M-RULE CONFORMING

### Pair 2: Zhun 屯 (#3) / Meng 蒙 (#4)
**KW order: Zhun first**

**Zhun (屯)** — Difficulty at the Beginning / Sprouting. The primal emergence from chaos — the first shoot pushing through frozen ground. Judgment: "Difficulty at the Beginning works supreme success... It furthers one to appoint helpers." The situation is raw potential encountering initial resistance.

**Meng (蒙)** — Youthful Folly / Inexperience / Covering. The state of not-yet-knowing, immaturity. Judgment: "Youthful Folly has success. It is not I who seek the young fool; the young fool seeks me." The situation is ignorance that requires instruction.

**Directionality: Zhun → Meng (clear).** The Xugua: "有天地然後萬物生焉... 物之始生，蒙" — After heaven and earth come into being, myriad things are born. Things at their birth are covered/ignorant." Zhun is birth itself (the sprouting); Meng is the condition of the newborn (not yet knowing). Birth precedes the need for education. The Zagua: "屯，見而不失其居。蒙，雜而著。" — "Zhun is visible but does not lose its place. Meng is mixed and apparent."

**Activity:** Zhun is more dynamic (struggling emergence), Meng is more passive (sitting in unknowing, waiting for the teacher).

**Confidence: Clear**
**Meaning-predicted order: Zhun first**

### Pair 7: Tong Ren 同人 (#13) / Da You 大有 (#14)
**KW order: Tong Ren first**

**Tong Ren (同人)** — Fellowship with Others / Gathering of People. Judgment: "Fellowship with Men in the open. Success. It furthers one to cross the great water." The situation is forming community, uniting with others for a common purpose.

**Da You (大有)** — Great Possession / Abundance. Judgment: "Great Possession. Supreme success." The situation is abundant wealth and resources — having much.

**Activity:** Tong Ren is more active (reaching out, building fellowship). Da You is more receptive (receiving abundance that comes from fellowship). Classic action → consequence.

**Confidence: Clear**
**Meaning-predicted order: Tong Ren first**

### Pair 13: Wu Wang 无妄 (#25) / Da Chu 大畜 (#26)
**KW order: Wu Wang first**

**Wu Wang (无妄)** — Innocence / Without Falsehood / The Unexpected. Judgment: "Innocence. Supreme success. Perseverance furthers. If someone is not as he should be, he has misfortune, and it does not further him to undertake anything." The situation is being aligned with the natural/correct — free from ulterior motives, acting spontaneously in accord with the Way.

**Da Chu (大畜)** — Great Taming / Great Accumulation. Judgment: "Great Taming. Perseverance furthers. Not eating at home brings good fortune. It furthers one to cross the great water." The situation is accumulating great resources, taming great forces, storing up virtue and knowledge.

**Directionality: Wu Wang → Da Chu (suggestive).** The Xugua: "有无妄然後可畜，故受之以大畜。" — "Only when there is Innocence can accumulation follow; therefore Great Taming follows." Innocence (purity of intention) is the prerequisite for genuine accumulation. You must first be free of falsehood before great resources can be properly gathered and held. Without innocence, accumulation becomes hoarding or corruption.

**Activity:** Wu Wang is more inward (alignment with the natural, spontaneous rightness). Da Chu is more outward (actively taming, accumulating, storing up). This pair has the pattern: inner condition → outer achievement.

**Confidence: Suggestive** — The Xugua's logic is clear, but the meanings alone (innocence vs. accumulation) don't have as strong a developmental arrow as birth→education or fellowship→abundance. One could argue that great taming *produces* the condition of innocence (discipline produces spontaneity). The Xugua resolves the ambiguity in favor of Wu Wang first, but the meanings are less overdetermined than the clearer pairs.

**Meaning-predicted order: Wu Wang first**

## UPPER CANON — NON-M-DECISIVE

### Pair 1: Qian 乾 (#1) / Kun 坤 (#2) [complement pair]
**KW order: Qian first**

**Qian (乾)** — The Creative / Heaven / Initiating. Judgment: "The Creative works sublime success, furthering through perseverance." The primal initiating force. Pure yang. The dragon rising through the stages of manifestation.

**Kun (坤)** — The Receptive / Earth / Responding. Judgment: "The Receptive brings about sublime success, furthering through the perseverance of a mare." The primal responsive force. Pure yin. The mare following, the earth receiving.

**Directionality: Qian → Kun (clear, cosmologically foundational).** This is THE primordial pair. The Xugua opens: "有天地然後萬物生焉" — "There are heaven and earth, and afterward the myriad things are born." Heaven before earth. Creative before receptive. Initiating before responding. This is the axiom from which the entire sequence proceeds.

The Zagua: "乾剛坤柔" — "Qian is hard/firm, Kun is soft/yielding." The Wenyan extensively discusses both, treating Qian as the source of beginning and Kun as the source of completion.

**Activity:** Qian is pure activity/initiation. Kun is pure receptivity/response. The paradigmatic case.

**Confidence: Clear**
**Meaning-predicted order: Qian first**

### Pair 3: Xu 需 (#5) / Song 訟 (#6)
**KW order: Xu first**

**Xu (需)** — Waiting / Nourishment / Attending. Judgment: "Waiting. If you are sincere, you have light and success. Perseverance brings good fortune. It furthers one to cross the great water." The situation is waiting for the right moment — patient anticipation of nourishment or fulfillment. The character 需 implies need/requirement as well as waiting.

**Song (訟)** — Conflict / Contention / Litigation. Judgment: "Conflict. You are sincere and are being obstructed. A cautious halt halfway brings good fortune. Going through to the end brings misfortune. It furthers one to see the great man. It does not further one to cross the great water." The situation is dispute, arguing over resources or rights.

**Directionality: Xu → Song (suggestive).** The Xugua: "飲食必有訟，故受之以訟。" — "Where there is drinking and eating [the nourishment of Xu], there must be contention; therefore Conflict follows." Waiting for nourishment leads to conflict over resources. The Xugua treats Song as a consequence of Xu's situation — when needs must be met (Xu), disputes over how to meet them arise (Song).

**Activity:** Xu is more passive/receptive (waiting, anticipating). Song is more active/confrontational (disputing, litigating). Condition (needing) → consequence (contending).

**Confidence: Suggestive** — The connection is logical but Song could equally be seen as *preceding* Xu: you conflict first, then you learn to wait. The Xugua's reasoning tips it, but the meanings alone are not as strongly directional.

**Meaning-predicted order: Xu first**

### Pair 11: Shi He 噬嗑 (#21) / Bi 賁 (#22)
**KW order: Shi He first**

**Shi He (噬嗑)** — Biting Through / Decisive Action. Judgment: "Biting Through has success. It is favorable to let justice be administered." The situation is forceful action to remove an obstruction — like biting through something stuck between the teeth. The Daxiang links it to law enforcement and punishment.

**Bi (賁)** — Grace / Adornment / Elegance. Judgment: "Grace has success. In small matters it is favorable to undertake something." The situation is beauty, cultural refinement, ornamentation — the aesthetic dimension of civilization. The Daxiang speaks of clarifying governance but not daring to decide great legal cases based on form alone.

**Directionality: Shi He → Bi (suggestive).** The Xugua: "嗑者合也。物不可以苟合而已，故受之以賁。" — "Shi He means union/biting together. Things should not be united carelessly; therefore Grace follows." After the forceful bringing-together (biting through the obstruction), adornment/proper form follows. Substance before style. Justice before grace.

**Activity:** Shi He is active/forceful (biting through, enforcing). Bi is more passive/aesthetic (adorning, refining). Force → refinement.

**Confidence: Suggestive** — The Xugua's logic works but the developmental link is less tight than pairs like Jian/Xie. One could argue that grace (setting proper form) should precede forceful action (which is the refinement that makes action appropriate). The Xugua resolves it, but the meanings alone are less determinative.

**Meaning-predicted order: Shi He first**

### Pair 27: Jian 漸 (#53) / Gui Mei 歸妹 (#54)
**KW order: Jian first**

**Jian (漸)** — Development / Gradual Progress / Infiltration. Judgment: "Development. The maiden is given in marriage. Good fortune. Perseverance furthers." The situation is slow, proper, step-by-step development — the correct form of marriage where the woman enters her husband's family through proper stages and rituals.

**Gui Mei (歸妹)** — The Marrying Maiden / The Younger Sister Given in Marriage. Judgment: "The Marrying Maiden. Undertakings bring misfortune. Nothing that would further." The situation is an improper or precipitous union — the younger sister married off as a secondary wife, an irregular arrangement that lacks the proper foundation.

**Directionality: Jian → Gui Mei (suggestive, with complexity).** The Xugua: "漸者進也。進必有所歸，故受之以歸妹。" — "Jian means advancing. Advance inevitably leads to belonging somewhere; therefore Marrying Maiden follows." Gradual development leads to the moment of commitment/belonging.

However, the meaning-level relationship is complex. Jian describes the *proper* way (gradual, correct marriage). Gui Mei describes the *improper* way (hasty, irregular marriage). They're not so much a developmental sequence as a contrast between right and wrong ways of achieving union. The Zagua treats them as contrasts: "漸，女歸待男行也。歸妹，女之終也。" — "Jian means the woman marries while awaiting the man's action. Gui Mei means the woman's conclusion."

One reading: proper development (Jian) is shown first, then its shadow/inversion (Gui Mei) follows — the right way, then the wrong way. Another reading: gradual is the process, arrival (even imperfect) is the outcome.

**Activity:** Jian is measured and patient (gradual steps). Gui Mei is more abrupt and compromised (the hasty arrangement). But neither maps cleanly onto active/receptive.

**Confidence: Suggestive** — The ordering works but the pair is more contrastive than developmental. The Xugua provides a logical link, but the hexagram meanings are more about right vs. wrong approach than about temporal sequence.

**Meaning-predicted order: Jian first**

Small things may be done; great things should not be done. The flying bird brings the message: It is not well to strive upward, it is well to remain below. Great good fortune." The situation is a time when small things exceed — you should attend to the small, the humble, the near, rather than reaching for the great.

**Directionality: Zhong Fu → Xiao Guo (suggestive).** The Xugua: "有其信者必行之，故受之以小過。" — "Those who have inner truth must put it into practice; therefore Small Exceeding follows." Having inner truth leads to its expression — but the expression is necessarily imperfect, exceeded by reality, and must remain humble (small).

**Activity:** Zhong Fu is inward (inner truth, sincerity at the core). Xiao Guo is outward but modest (small acts, staying low, attending to the nearby). Inner truth → humble expression.

**Confidence: Suggestive** — The Xugua provides a connection but this is one of the more tenuous developmental sequences. Inner truth and small exceeding are not obviously connected by the hexagram meanings alone. The complement-pair structure (these are structural complements) may matter more than the developmental logic.

**Meaning-predicted order: Zhong Fu first**

### Pair 32: Ji Ji 既濟 (#63) / Wei Ji 未濟 (#64) [complement pair]
**KW order: Ji Ji first**

**Ji Ji (既濟)** — After Completion / Already Across / Already Fording. Judgment: "After Completion. Success in small matters. Perseverance furthers. At the beginning good fortune, at the end disorder." The situation is: everything is in its right place, the crossing has been made, the task is done. But — the warning — perfection is unstable. What is complete begins to unravel.

**Wei Ji (未濟)** — Before Completion / Not Yet Across / Not Yet Fording. Judgment: "Before Completion. Success. But if the little fox, after nearly completing the crossing, gets his tail in the water, there is nothing that would further." The situation is: the task is not yet done, the arrangement is not yet perfect, the crossing is still underway. Potential remains unrealized.

**Directionality: Ji Ji → Wei Ji (clear, and profoundly significant).**

This is the *final* pair in the entire sequence, and the ordering is one of the most discussed in all of Yijing commentary. The Xugua: "物不可窮也，故受之以未濟終焉。" — "Things cannot be exhausted; therefore Before Completion ends [the sequence]."

The ordering is deeply intentional: completion comes *before* incompletion. The sequence ends not with closure but with openness. Ji Ji = everything in place, but "at the end, disorder." Wei Ji = not yet in place, but full of potential. The tradition reads this as the ultimate statement of the Yi's philosophy: **nothing is ever truly finished; completion itself generates the conditions for a new beginning.**

**Activity:** Ji Ji is settled/complete (everything in its place — a kind of stasis). Wei Ji is unsettled/in-process (things still moving toward their places — potential energy). In a sense, Ji Ji is more "receptive" (resting in completion) and Wei Ji is more "active" (still striving toward order). But the primary logic is not active/receptive — it is finished→unfinished, which paradoxically means stable→dynamic, closed→open.

The Zagua: "既濟，定也。未濟，男之窮也。" — "Ji Ji means settled/determined. Wei Ji means the male/yang exhausted." (This last phrase is debated in the tradition.)

**Confidence: Clear** — The final pair's ordering is treated as one of the most meaningful features of the entire sequence. Every major commentator discusses why the sequence ends with "Not Yet" rather than "Already."

**Meaning-predicted order: Ji Ji first**

### Observations

**1. No pair produced "no prediction."** Every pair had at least a suggestive directional reading from the traditional texts.

**2. The "Suggestive" pairs cluster in specific categories:**
- Complement pairs tend to be weaker: Kan/Li, Yi/Da Guo, Zhong Fu/Xiao Guo
- Pairs where both members describe *conditions* rather than one describing a condition and the other its resolution: Xu/Song, Xiao Chu/Lü
- Pairs where the developmental logic requires the Xugua to be explicit: Wu Wang/Da Chu, Dun/Da Zhuang, Jian/Gui Mei

**3. The unifying principle is confirmed as "developmental priority" — not "active first."** The first member is whatever creates the conditions for the second, whether that is:
- Active initiation (Ge→Ding, Zhen→Gen, Guai→Gou)
- Passive suffering (Bo→Fu, Jian→Xie, Kun→Jing)
- Cosmic primacy (Qian→Kun)
- Moral prerequisite (Wu Wang→Da Chu, Qian/Modesty→Yu)
- Cyclical priority (Tai→Pi, Jin→Ming Yi, Feng→Lü)

**4. The Xugua confound remains.** The Xugua was written to explain the existing sequence, so it will always agree with KW. The stronger test is whether the hexagram meanings *without* the Xugua predict the ordering. For the 22 "Clear" pairs, the answer is yes — the developmental logic is overdetermined by the hexagram names, judgments, and images. For the 10 "Suggestive" pairs, the Xugua is doing more of the work, and the meanings alone would produce a weaker or ambiguous prediction.

**5. Zero contradictory cases found.** Not a single pair showed a meaning-level argument for the *reverse* ordering that was stronger than the argument for the KW ordering. The closest are Kan/Li (cosmological co-equality) and Xun/Dui (trigram poles without strong ordering logic), but even these default to KW's direction under examination.
