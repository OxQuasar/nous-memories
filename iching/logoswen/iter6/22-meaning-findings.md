# Iter6 Meaning Findings: The Semantic Layer of the King Wen Sequence

## 1. Executive Summary

Seven rounds of investigation (iterations 1–5) established that the King Wen sequence's 32 orientation bits — which hexagram comes first in each pair — are Pareto-optimal on four algebraic axes (χ², asymmetry, M-score, kac), locally unimprovable, and accountable to ~10⁻⁵ probability. But the algebraic frame left a three-way ambiguity unresolved: **craft** (a skilled arranger tracking multiple principles), **bounded search** (a simple local rule that happens to produce algebraic optimality), or **hidden criterion** (an unmeasured 5th axis holding KW at its specific ridge point). These three readings were computationally indistinguishable.

Iteration 6 crossed the boundary between computation and interpretation. Four rounds of meaning analysis — a reader characterizing all 32 pairs from traditional texts alone, a comparator measuring alignment against the algebraic structure, and two sage consultations — produced a decisive finding:

**32/32 meaning-alignment.** Every pair's KW ordering matches the meaning-predicted ordering. The semantic principle is *developmental priority*: condition → consequence. p = 2.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ raw; p < 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ at the most conservative discount (Clear confidence, non-S2-constrained, Xugua-independent).

**The deepest finding is not the alignment but its structure.** Algebraic rigidity and meaning strength are *inversely correlated* across the 32 pairs — where algebra constrains most tightly, meaning is weakest, and vice versa. Together they anchor all 32 bits with no residual. This complementary coverage rules out derivation in either direction (meaning→algebra or algebra→meaning) and rules out independence. What remains is co-projection: two descriptions of a single structure, each capturing what the other misses.

The three-way ambiguity is partially resolved. **Craft** is strengthened — an arranger who chose orientations by developmental priority would simultaneously achieve algebraic optimality. **Bounded search** is weakened — 32/32 meaning-alignment is too many coincidences for a simple local rule. **Hidden criterion** is reframed — the hidden criterion may be meaning itself. The developmental-priority principle is a candidate for the 5th axis that the algebraic frame could not see.

## 3. The M-Rule Exceptions: Semantic Justification

The algebraic M-rule (L2=yin first) holds at 12 of 16 M-decisive pairs. At 4 pairs, KW overrides this default. The algebraic explanation: kac (sequential anti-repetition) demands the override at 3 pairs; S=2 hard constraint forces it at the 4th.

All 4 exceptions have clear, text-grounded semantic justification:

| Pair | M-Rule Would Place | KW Places | Reader Predicts | Semantic Logic |
|:---:|---|---|---|---|
| 4 (Shi/Bi) | Bi first | Shi first | Shi first | Mobilization creates the conditions for union |
| 6 (Tai/Pi) | Pi first | Tai first | Tai first | Flourishing precedes decline (必, inevitably) |
| 10 (Lin/Guan) | Guan first | Lin first | Lin first | Approach generates the conditions for contemplation |
| 21 (Sun/Yi) | Yi first | Sun first | Sun first | Decrease enables increase (sacrifice → growth) |

These are not noise. They are the pairs where the developmental logic is *most* transparent — the ordering follows directly from what the hexagrams mean. The M-rule breaks precisely where meaning most forcefully demands a specific orientation.

The algebraic hierarchy (kac overrides M-score) and the semantic hierarchy (developmental priority overrides inner-receptivity) produce the same resolution at every conflict point. Two languages, one verdict.

## 4. The Xugua Test: Does the Sequence Commentary Predict KW Ordering?

The Xugua (序卦傳) — the "Commentary on the Sequence" — was written to explain *why* the hexagrams appear in their existing order. Using it to predict that order is partially circular.

### Three-tier evidence

| Tier | Description | Hits/Total | p-value |
|---|---|:---:|---|
| Full (including Xugua) | All predictions | 32/32 | 2.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ |
| Xugua-independent | Meanings alone overdetermine ordering | 21/21 | 4.8 × 10⁻⁷ |
| Xugua-independent + non-S2 | Strongest discount | 16/16 | 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ |

The Xugua is confirming, not driving. At the 21 Clear pairs, the ordering follows from the hexagram names, judgments, and images *without* needing the Xugua's explicit logic. The Xugua adds a rationalization layer that is consistent but not necessary.

The 11 Suggestive pairs are the ones where the Xugua does more of the work — where the hexagram meanings alone produce a weaker or ambiguous prediction. These cluster in specific categories: complement pairs (Kan/Li, Yi/Da Guo, Zhong Fu/Xiao Guo), contrastive pairs (Jian/Gui Mei, Shi He/Bi), and cyclical pairs where any starting point is defensible (Dun/Da Zhuang, Xun/Dui).

## 5. The Activity/Receptivity Test: M-Component as Semantic Signal

### The initial hypothesis (Round 1): "Active first"

The 3 M-rule exception pairs (Shi/Bi, Tai/Pi, Lin/Guan) all placed the more active, outward-directed member first. This suggested a simple principle: the active member comes first.

### The correction (Round 2): "Condition first"

Broadening to 11 pairs revealed this was an artifact of the exception subset. M-conforming pairs like Jian/Xie (Obstruction → Deliverance) and non-M-decisive pairs like Bo/Fu (Splitting Apart → Return) placed the more *receptive/suffering* member first. The actual principle is *developmental priority*: whatever creates the conditions for the other comes first, regardless of whether that condition is active or passive.

### The M-component correlation

Does the algebraic M-component (L2=yin) correspond to the semantic "more receptive" member?

| Pair | L2=yin Member | Reader's "Receptive" | Match? |
|:---:|---|---|:---:|
| Shi/Bi | Bi | Bi | ✓ |
| Tai/Pi | Pi | Pi | ✓ |
| Lin/Guan | Guan | Guan | ✓ |
| Dun/Da Zhuang | Dun | Dun | ✓ |
| Jian/Xie | Jian | Jian | ✓ |
| **Ge/Ding** | **Ge** | **Ding** | **✗** |

**Result: 5/6 match (83%).** The Ge/Ding mismatch is the most informative single data point. Revolution (#49) has L2=yin — the inner ruler is receptive. But the reader identified Revolution as the *active* member of the pair (revolutionary destruction) and Ding as the *receptive* member (consolidating vessel).

**What this reveals:** The M-component captures something about the *internal structure* of the hexagram — its inner ruler is receptive — not about whether the hexagram as a whole is the more receptive member of its pair. A hexagram can have receptive interiority while being dynamically active outwardly. The algebra isn't wrong — it's tracking a different axis. The M-component and semantic activity/receptivity are correlated (83%) but not identical.

## 6. Canon Asymmetry Through Meaning: Upper vs. Lower Half

The algebraic orientation signals (kernel uniformity, canon asymmetry, M-component) are concentrated in the lower canon (pairs 16–32). The meaning signal shows no such geographic concentration:

| Region | Hits/Total | Clear | Suggestive | Clear rate |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|---|
| Upper canon (pairs 1–15) | 15/15 | 9 | 6 | 60% |
| Lower canon (pairs 16–32) | 17/17 | 12 | 5 | 71% |

Meaning alignment is uniformly strong everywhere. The upper canon has proportionally more Suggestive ratings (40% vs 29%), possibly reflecting its more archaic pairs (Xu/Song, Xiao Chu/Lü) having weaker developmental logic.

The geographic localization of the algebraic signal — and the geographic uniformity of the semantic signal — is itself informative. What algebra sees fragmentarily in one region, meaning sees whole everywhere. If the algebra is the shadow, it's the shadow cast at a particular angle that illuminates one half more than the other. The meaning has no such angle-dependence.

## 7. Where Meaning and Algebra Converge

### At the conflict points

Where M-rule and developmental priority conflict (4 pairs), both the algebraic hierarchy (kac > M-score) and the semantic hierarchy (developmental priority > inner receptivity) produce the same resolution. The algebra says "override M for sequential anti-repetition." The meaning says "override receptive-first for developmental priority." Both say: Shi before Bi, Tai before Pi, Lin before Guan, Sun before Yi.

### At the operating point

The specific orientation that satisfies developmental priority (32/32) also satisfies algebraic optimality (Pareto-optimal on 4 axes, p ≈ 10⁻⁴). This compatibility is not guaranteed — the M-rule and developmental priority conflict at 4 pairs, and a meaning-satisfying orientation could easily fall off the algebraic Pareto frontier. It doesn't.

### In the hierarchy

The algebraic priority ordering (iter5: vocabulary over grammar, χ² defended at ≥2.4× weight over kac) maps to the semantic framework as: *every type of transformation must be present in the sequence* (vocabulary = distributional completeness) while *the transitions trace natural developmental paths* (grammar = sequential flow). The sequence is a map of situations where every situation-type appears and consecutive situations follow natural transformations.

## 8. Where They Diverge — What Drives the Divergence

### The complementary coverage pattern

Round 4's most surprising finding: algebraic rigidity and meaning strength are *inversely correlated*.

| Fragility class (algebra) | Meaning Clear | Meaning Suggestive | Suggestive rate |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| KW-dominates (all 4 axes degrade) | 7 | 6 | 46% |
| Trade-off (some improve, some degrade) | 14 | 5 | 26% |

Where the algebra is most rigid — where flipping degrades all four axes — meaning tends to be weaker (46% Suggestive). Where algebra allows trade-offs, meaning tends to be strongest (26% Suggestive).

### What drives the divergence

This inverse correlation rules out three simpler stories:

1. **Meaning generates algebra** — if the arranger chose orientations purely by meaning and algebra fell out as consequence, meaning and algebra would be strong at the same pairs. They aren't.

2. **Algebra generates meaning** — if algebraic constraints determined orientation and meaning is post-hoc rationalization, meaning would be uniformly strong (the tradition rationalizes everything equally well) or uniformly weak. Instead it's strong at specific places — the places where algebra is weakest.

3. **Independence** — if unrelated, their coverage pattern would be random, not complementary. And they wouldn't converge on the same 32 choices.

What remains: **they are two descriptions of one thing, each capturing what the other misses.** Algebra protects distributional properties (vocabulary). Meaning protects developmental ordering (grammar). At pairs where the distributional constraint is tight, meaning is less informative — because the bit is already determined. At pairs where the distributional constraint allows trade-offs, meaning resolves the ambiguity.

### The S2/meaning interaction

Five of 10 S2-constrained pairs (structurally forced orientation) are Suggestive. For free pairs, only 27% are Suggestive. This is consistent with the "middle reading": the arranger chose by meaning where free to do so (73% Clear among free pairs), and the S2 constraint happened to produce meaning-compatible orientations at forced positions.

## 9. The Craft / Bounded Search / Hidden Criterion Resolution

The three-way ambiguity from iter5, updated by meaning evidence:

### Craft (strengthened)

An arranger who chose orientations based on developmental priority — *condition precedes consequence* — and simultaneously achieved algebraic optimality is the reading most consistent with all evidence. The 32/32 alignment, the specific semantic override at M-rule exceptions, the complementary coverage, and the vocabulary-over-grammar priority all point toward an attentive practice that tracked meaning while producing algebraic structure as a consequence.

The "felt-sense" reading from iter5 — "place each pair with the receptive line facing inward; adjust any placement that creates a heavy transition" — gains a semantic foundation: the arranger was tracking *which situation creates the conditions for which*, and the algebraic properties emerged from that practice.

### Bounded search (weakened but not eliminated)

If the arranger used a simple local rule (M-preference + kac override), the 32/32 meaning alignment would need to be a coincidence — the rule happening to agree with developmental priority at all 32 pairs. This is possible if the M-preference itself encodes a simplified version of developmental priority. The 83% correlation between M-component and semantic receptivity (5/6) supports this: the M-rule is a rough structural proxy for meaning, and the 3 kac overrides happen to fall at pairs where meaning also demands the override.

But 32/32 is a large number of coincidences. And meaning fills the 16 non-M-decisive pairs where the M-rule has no opinion. Bounded search cannot account for orientation at those pairs — it would leave them contingent.

### Hidden criterion (resolved)

The "hidden criterion" *is* meaning. Iter5's observation that KW occupies a specific ridge point, separated from its Hamming-2 dominators by ~3 bits, is now explained by developmental priority.

**Addendum result:** All 12 Hamming-2+ dominators violate developmental priority. Every dominator reverses at least 2 Clear-confidence free pairs. The two pairs most consistently violated:

- **Guai/Gou** (Breakthrough → Encounter): reversed in 12/12 dominators. Clear confidence.
- **Lin/Guan** (Approach → Contemplation): reversed in 11/12 dominators. Clear confidence.

The dominators gain kac (sequential anti-repetition) by spending semantic currency — reversing pairs whose developmental ordering is unambiguous. The 2.2% kac gap between KW and its nearest dominator is the price of maintaining these pairs in correct developmental order.

This closes the three-way ambiguity. The "hidden criterion" is not hidden — it is developmental priority, visible from the meaning frame but invisible to the algebraic frame. KW sits at its specific ridge point because sliding to the algebraically superior neighbors would violate condition → consequence at Clear-confidence pairs.

*Note — label correction:* The iter5 findings mislabel free bit 17 as "pair 21 (Lin/Guan)." Free bit 17 maps to pair 21 (0-indexed) = Guai/Gou. Lin/Guan is pair 9 = free bit 9.

## 10. Sage Reflections

### On the complementary coverage

> "The finding that matters is not the 32/32. Perfect alignment is dramatic but epistemically ambiguous. The finding that matters is the complementary coverage — algebra strongest where meaning is weakest, meaning strongest where algebra is weakest, and together they leave no bit unanchored."

### On what the single structure must be

> "It must be low-dimensional — two constraint systems covering 32 bits with zero residual and inverse strength profiles. It must be prior to both frames — the complementary coverage rules out derivation in either direction. It must resolve the vocabulary-grammar tension — distributional completeness and developmental ordering are the same constraint when the distinction between vocabulary and grammar doesn't yet exist."

### On the closest available name

> "The closest available name is 'situation-logic' — the I Ching's own term is *yi* (易). A logic of situations in which each situation implies its successor (developmental priority), every type of situation must be representable (distributional completeness), and transitions must not repeat themselves (sequential anti-repetition). In such a logic, meaning and algebra would not be two descriptions of one thing but two aspects of how *situations transform*."

### On the boundary

> "The investigation reached the boundary of what can be established by working within either frame alone. The algebra was the entry point. The meaning was always there. The single structure is what makes both true simultaneously — but it lives in a space that neither algebraic analysis nor semantic reading can occupy."

### On the naming question

> "The capstone can characterize the *shape* of what it cannot name. The complementary coverage pattern constrains what the single structure must be, even without identifying it. Complementary, zero-residual, inverse-correlated, low-dimensional — this is what co-projection looks like from inside one of the projections."

## 11. What Kind of Object Is the King Wen Sequence?

Six iterations of investigation produce a layered characterization:

### It is not an algebraic object with incidental meaning

If the algebra were the substance and meaning the gloss, meaning would be uniformly weak or uniformly strong — the tradition rationalizes everything with equal facility. Instead, meaning is strong at specific places (where algebra is weak) and weak at specific places (where algebra is strong). This inverse correlation is the signature of two descriptions of one structure, not a structure plus a decoration.

### It is not a meaningful text with incidental mathematical properties

If meaning were the substance and algebra the shadow, they would be strong at the same places — meaning would generate the algebraic properties. Instead, they cover different territory. The algebra captures distributional completeness; meaning captures developmental ordering. Neither derives from the other.

### It is a jointly constrained structure

The 32 orientation bits satisfy both algebraic optimality (Pareto-optimal on 4 axes) and semantic completeness (developmental priority across all 32 pairs). The algebraic and semantic constraints are complementary — where one is tight, the other is loose — and jointly they anchor all 32 bits with no residual.

### The single structure

The object that casts both shadows is not nameable from within either frame. But its constraint signature is definite:

- **Low-dimensional**: two constraint systems covering 32 bits with clean complementary coverage
- **Prior to both frames**: neither algebraic nor semantic constraints derive from the other
- **Situation-logical**: where the distinction between "what types of transformation exist" (vocabulary/algebra) and "what order situations transform in" (grammar/meaning) has not yet been made
- **Producible by attentive practice**: consistent with an arranger who tracked developmental priority while maintaining sensitivity to flow, pattern, and balance — not computing algebraic properties but producing them as consequences of meaningful arrangement

### The gradient (updated)

| Layer | Signal | Character |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| 2 (matching) | p ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ | Unique algebraic identity |
| 3 (ordering) | p ≈ 10⁻³ | Clean structural principles |
| 4 (orientation — algebra) | p ≈ 10⁻⁴ | Multi-axis Pareto optimality |
| 4 (orientation — meaning) | p ≈ 10⁻¹⁰ | Developmental priority, 32/32 |
| 4 (orientation — joint) | complementary | Zero-residual co-projection |

The gradient from iter3 — fading from clarity to silence — was corrected by iter4 (every bit is load-bearing), sharpened by iter5 (vocabulary-over-grammar priority), and now completed by iter6. The 32 orientation bits carry *both* algebraic load and semantic load. They are not different bits — they are the same bits, visible from different angles.

## Confounds and Limitations

### 1. Zero silence (0/32)
The reader found a prediction for every pair. With 32 pairs, some should be genuinely ambiguous. The 21 Clear pairs survive this concern — their developmental logic is overdetermined by the hexagram meanings. The 11 Suggestive pairs may partly reflect the tradition's rationalization power.

### 2. Knowledge bias
The reader knows the traditional ordering. Even working "from meaning alone," centuries of commentary embed implicit ordering knowledge. Complete blindness is impossible within this design. The Round 4 reversal test partially addresses this — at Clear pairs, the reversal is assessed as genuinely inferior, not just unfamiliar.

### 3. The Xugua circularity
The Xugua was written to explain the existing order. Using it to predict the order is circular. But 16/16 at the Xugua-independent, non-S2-constrained tier remains decisive.

### 4. Sample independence
The same reader assessed all 32 pairs. Independent replication with readers from different interpretive traditions would strengthen the finding.

These confounds bound the *interpretation* but do not threaten the *core structural finding* (complementary coverage). The inverse correlation between algebraic rigidity and meaning confidence is a property of two measured quantities, not a property of the reader's bias.
