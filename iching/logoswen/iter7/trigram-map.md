## The Question

The KW sequence has been mapped from two sides — Z₂⁶ algebra (iters 1–5) and traditional meaning (iter6). Both account for the 32 orientation bits with complementary coverage. Iter7 asked: **does the intermediate space — where bit patterns become trigram interactions (inner × outer) — reveal the mechanism of co-projection?**

**Answer: Yes.** The trigram layer has its own architecture. It is the **joint** — not where algebra and meaning meet, but where they are constrained to move together while remaining distinct. The architecture is: a selection membrane at the trigram boundary, two independent continuity modes that tile the complementary coverage, and a permission-selection pattern that repeats at every scale.

## The Eight Findings

### 1. The Cross-Trigram Theorem (Round 1)

Every mirror-pair generator changes bits in both the lower and upper trigram. No KW pair can share a trigram. Pairs that are algebraically close (same orbit) are always trigram-distant (different row AND column of the 8×8 grid).

This forces each pair to be a *full situational inversion* — different inner AND outer — the structural permission for developmental priority to operate as a global principle.

### 2. M's Symmetric Uniqueness (Round 1)

Among the three single generators (O, M, I), M is the only one that flips the *same* position (middle bit, the ruler line) in both trigrams. O and I cross positions (0↔2). This is why M-component = nuclear trigram rule — the same structure, named differently by the two frames. M's symmetric action on the ruler line is *why* this component carries meaning so transparently.

### 3. The Kernel = Id Theorem (Round 2)

**A bridge can preserve a trigram only if its kernel dressing is id** — no palindromic component. Each kernel generator straddles the trigram boundary; any active one forces both trigrams to change.

Of 31 bridges: 15 have kernel = id. Of those 15: 9 preserve, 6 are near-preserving (min_dist = 1). The gradient is smooth.

### 4. The Trigram Boundary Theorem (Round 5)

**Complete characterization:** an id-kernel bridge preserves a trigram if and only if all its XOR 1-bits fall on one side of the trigram boundary (all in L1–L3 or all in L4–L6).

Two-level geometric gate:
- **Level 1** (kernel = id): no mirror pair flips both members → no symmetric straddling
- **Level 2** (one-sided XOR): remaining unpaired bits all on one side → no asymmetric straddling

The trigram boundary (L3|L4) is a **selection membrane**. The mirror-pair decomposition straddles it — every mirror pair has one member on each side. The trigram decomposition is separated by it. Preservation requires all changes to stay on one side. The membrane is where the two decompositions interact.

Three classes of bridges:

| Condition | Count | Character |
|-----------|:-----:|-----------|
| kernel = id AND one-sided | 9 | **Preserving** |
| kernel = id AND split | 6 | **Near-preserving** |
| kernel ≠ id | 16 | **Both change** |

The 6 non-preserving id-kernel bridges split into:
- **Structurally locked** (B1, B8): cannot become preserving under any single orientation flip
- **Orientation-selected** (B22, B24): preservation available but rejected — developmental priority chose otherwise
- **Content-locked** (B10, B31): locked by pair content, not orientation

The spoiler bit pattern: 5/6 spoiler bits at position 0 (bottom of trigram). The ruler line (position 1, M's line) never spoils — M's symmetric action cannot produce a stray bit on the wrong side.

### 5. The Lag-4 Periodicity (Round 3, p = 0.0017)

Every second pair tends to share a trigram, creating **corridors** of partial trigram continuity. The strongest: Earth spanning pairs 4–10 (positions 7–20), Heaven spanning pairs 1–5 (positions 1–9).

### 6. Corridors Are a Pair-Ordering Property (Round 4)

Shuffling pair order destroys corridors (p = 0.014). Randomizing orientation preserves them (p = 0.21). This is the first identified structural property of the pair *ordering* itself — a different degree of freedom from the 32 orientation bits studied in iters 1–6.

### 7. Two Independent Continuity Modes Partition Complementary Coverage (Round 4)

| Mode | Scale | Source | Where | Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Preserving bridges** | Local (pair→pair) | Orientation choice | Trade-off pairs (67%) | Meaning strong |
| **Corridors** | Periodic (every 2nd pair) | Pair ordering | KW-dom pairs (46%) | Algebra strong |

Zero overlap. Each provides structure where the other frame is weakest.

### 8. Permission-Selection at Every Level (All Rounds)

| Level | Permission (algebraic) | Selection |
|---|---|---|
| Bridge preservation | kernel = id + one-sidedness | 9 of 15 permitted bridges preserve |
| Corridor structure | Pair ordering adjacency | Specific pairs create Earth/Heaven arcs |
| Orientation | S2-free constraint space | Developmental priority (32/32) |

B22 and B24 confirm that developmental priority and trigram preservation are **genuinely independent** — peer principles that sometimes conflict, not a hierarchy where one overrides the other.

## The Structural Picture

### One object, two readings

The hexagram has 6 bits. The mirror-pair decomposition groups them by symmetry (L1↔L6, L2↔L5, L3↔L4). The trigram decomposition groups them by position (L1-L3 | L4-L6). These two cuts are maximally non-aligned: every mirror pair straddles the trigram boundary. This non-alignment is not a defect or a tension — it is the structure.

There is no third thing joining two separate structures. The two decompositions were never separate. They are two ways of reading one object. The constraint is not imposed; it is constitutive.

### The membrane

The trigram boundary (L3|L4) is where the two readings interact. The trigram boundary theorem locates exactly this interaction: preservation requires zero straddling — neither symmetric (kernel) nor asymmetric (orbit delta) — across the membrane. This is a geometric fact about Z₂⁶ that has meaning-level consequences because the two readings correspond to the hexagram's two natures (algebraic pattern and situational meaning).

### The two construction layers

The King Wen sequence has (at least) two independently structured degrees of freedom:

1. **Pair ordering** (31 choices): carries corridor structure, lag-4 periodicity, macro-scale thematic arcs
2. **Orientation** (32 choices): carries kac, χ², M-score, developmental priority, bridge preservation

These interact — corridors cluster at KW-dom positions, bridges cluster at trade-off positions — but are not reducible to each other. Randomizing one preserves the other's signal.

### Co-projection seen from the joint

The unnamed structure — the thing that casts both the algebraic and semantic shadows — is the hexagram itself, read two ways. From the trigram layer, what iter6 called "complementary coverage" becomes visible as mechanism: the pair ordering creates structural scaffolding (corridors) at positions where algebraic constraint already holds the orientation bits, and the orientation choices create narrative continuity (preserving bridges) at positions where the pair ordering provides no scaffolding.

The algebra doesn't generate the meaning. The meaning doesn't generate the algebra. They are two readings of one object — and the sequence is built so that where one reading is most constrained, the other is most free, and vice versa.

## What Is Not Complete

The trigram map is structurally complete for bridge preservation and corridor structure. Three things are not:

1. **Pair ordering has no meaning-level characterization.** Developmental priority characterizes orientation (32 bits). Corridors characterize pair ordering structurally (31 choices). But *why* these pairs are neighbors — what developmental logic governs pair sequencing — is unmapped. This is the largest remaining degree of freedom.

2. **Corridor meanings are unread.** The Earth corridor's semantic arc was described. The Thunder/Mountain, Wind, and Lake corridors were not. Reading them would test whether corridors carry thematic content (like preserving bridges do) or are purely structural scaffolding.

3. **The nuclear layer is underexploited.** The nuclear contraction (64 hexagrams → 16 nuclear pair types) was noted but not investigated as a path. The KW path through nuclear space might reveal structure invisible at full resolution.
