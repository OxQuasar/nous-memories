# King Wen Sequence: Investigation Synthesis — Iters 4–8

## Relation to Synthesis-0

Synthesis-0 covers iters 1–3: the algebraic decomposition (Z₂⁶ = orbit × position), four layers (matching, ordering, orientation), 12 theorems, and the gradient from clarity to silence. It established the core machinery and found three marginal orientation signals (kernel uniformity, canon asymmetry, M-component) with ~19 of 27 free bits silent. It closed with two open pictures: algebra as substance or algebra as shadow of meaning.

Iters 4–8 resolved this ambiguity.

---

## What Changed

### The gradient narrative was wrong

Synthesis-0 described a gradient from clarity (Layer 2, p ≈ 10⁻¹⁷) to silence (19 residual bits), interpreted as fading structure — the arranger's attention diminishing at finer scales. Iter4 corrected this. The gradient was a frame artifact. Every one of the 27 free bits is load-bearing; none is silent. The structure doesn't fade — it was being measured with incomplete instruments.

### The three-way ambiguity was resolved

Synthesis-0 left three origin hypotheses indistinguishable: craft, bounded search, hidden criterion. Iters 5–6 resolved this. The hidden criterion is developmental priority — a meaning-level principle invisible to the algebraic frame. The bounded search hypothesis (simple local rule producing algebraic optimality by accident) is dead at 32/32 meaning-alignment.

### The "wild possibility" was confirmed

Synthesis-0 speculated that the algebra might be a shadow of semantic structure — that the 19 silent bits carry meaning the algebraic frame can't see. This is now established. The orientation bits encode developmental priority (condition → consequence) across all 32 pairs. Algebra and meaning are complementary descriptions of the same 32 bits, with inverse strength profiles and zero residual. The algebra is not wrong, but it is a projection.

## Iter4: The Constraint Surface (Fragility Mapping)

### The 4th axis: kernel autocorrelation

Synthesis-0 measured three orientation signals: χ² (kernel distributional uniformity), asymmetry (canon ordering preference), M-score (L2=yin preference). Iter4's structural census of 80 properties discovered a fourth: **kernel autocorrelation (kac)**, measuring sequential anti-repetition of bridge kernel dressings. KW's kac = −0.464, at the 0th percentile of S=2-free orientations — consecutive bridge kernels almost never repeat. This is structurally independent of χ² (which measures the histogram shape, not the sequence order).

### Pareto optimality

With four axes {χ², asymmetry, m-score, kac}, KW is **Pareto-optimal at Hamming distance 1**: no single-bit flip improves any axis without degrading another. This is not an optimization claim — it's a constraint claim. All 27 free bits are committed. The 19 "silent" bits from iter3 are not silent; they are load-bearing for axes that iter3 didn't measure. The gradient from clarity to silence was a measurement artifact.

**Note (from later sequence investigation):** Under a narrower criterion (degradation on f1 = mean consecutive kernel distance alone), only 16/32 bits are load-bearing. The 27/27 count holds specifically for the 4-axis Pareto criterion. The correct null model also deflates several original metrics — see `spaceprobe/sequence-characterization.md` for full corrections.

### The additivity hierarchy

Multi-bit analysis revealed that χ² is partially epistatic (two individually-improving flips can cancel when combined), while kac is approximately additive. This creates a hierarchy: the system defends χ² (distributional completeness) at ≥2.4× the weight it assigns to kac (sequential diversity). Vocabulary over grammar.

## Iter5: The Generative Question

### N=2

What principle, applied during construction, produces all four signals? Fifty candidate generators were tested. No single principle succeeds. The minimum is **N=2**: M-preference (L2=yin first, per-pair default) + sequential anti-repetition (kac override at conflict positions). These two principles jointly produce orientations that are Pareto-competitive on all four axes.

χ² emerges as a byproduct — not directly targeted but produced as a consequence of distributional diversity. Asymmetry is a partial residual — the weakest signal, requiring its own mechanism or arising as a substrate effect of the other two.

### The ridge point

KW is not globally Pareto-optimal. Twelve orientations at Hamming distance 2+ dominate it — matching or exceeding KW on all four axes. All 12 exploit the same mechanism: **χ² epistasis**. Two individually χ²-improving flips cancel when combined, producing a χ²-neutral, kac-improving move.

The escape gateway: **free bit 17** (pair 21, Guai/Gou) appears in all 12 dominators. Its near-zero kac cost makes it the tangent direction along the Pareto ridge. Combined with any of 12 partner bits, the epistatic interaction opens the escape.

KW sits at a ridge point: locally optimal in 24 dimensions, on a gentle slope in 3 (bits 9, 17, 23). The 2.2% kac gap between KW and its nearest dominator is small enough to be a construction fingerprint — consistent with single-pair perturbation sensitivity.

### The construction fingerprint

The investigation resolved three candidate explanations for the gap:
1. A hidden 5th algebraic axis — possible but weakening
2. Process limitation — the arranger couldn't detect 2-bit cooperative improvements
3. Meaning — the flipped bits violate semantic constraints

Iter6 settled this.

## Iter6: The Semantic Door

### 32/32 developmental priority

A reader agent characterized all 32 pairs from traditional I Ching texts (hexagram names, judgments, images, Xugua, commentators). A comparator agent measured alignment with KW's actual ordering.

**Result: 32/32.** Every pair's KW ordering matches the meaning-predicted ordering. The semantic principle is **developmental priority**: whatever creates the conditions for the other comes first — whether that condition is active (Revolution), passive (Obstruction), normative (Family), or suffering (Splitting Apart). p = 2.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ raw; p < 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ at the most conservative discount.

This is not "active first" or "receptive first." It operates across multiple registers: peak→decline, problem→resolution, discipline→fruit, spark→endurance.

### The M-rule exceptions

At 4 of 16 M-decisive pairs, KW overrides the M-preference (L2=yin first). All 4 have clear, text-grounded semantic justification. Where M-component and developmental priority conflict, KW follows meaning. The algebraic hierarchy (kac overrides M-score) independently produces the same resolution. Two languages, one verdict at every conflict point.

### Complementary coverage

The deepest finding of iter6: algebraic rigidity and meaning strength are **inversely correlated** across the 32 pairs. Where algebra constrains most tightly (KW-dominates — all four axes degrade under any flip), meaning is weakest (46% Suggestive). Where algebra allows trade-offs, meaning is strongest (26% Suggestive). Together they anchor all 32 bits with no residual.

This rules out three simpler stories:
- Meaning generates algebra → they'd be strong at the same places
- Algebra generates meaning → meaning would be uniformly strong or weak
- Independence → coverage would be random, not complementary

What remains: **co-projection**. Two descriptions of one structure, each capturing what the other misses.

### The dominator test (addendum)

All 12 Hamming-2+ dominators violate developmental priority. Every dominator reverses at least 2 Clear-confidence pairs. The universal cost: Guai/Gou (Breakthrough → Encounter) reversed in 12/12, Lin/Guan (Approach → Contemplation) reversed in 11/12.

The hidden criterion holding KW at its ridge point is not a 5th algebraic axis. It is **developmental priority** — visible from the meaning frame, invisible from the algebraic frame. The 2.2% kac gap is the price of maintaining correct developmental ordering.

*(Note: the iter5 findings mislabel free bit 17 as "Lin/Guan." Free bit 17 → pair index 21 (0-indexed) = Guai/Gou. Lin/Guan is pair 9 = free bit 9.)*

## Iter7: The Trigram Bridge

### The intermediate space

Each hexagram's six bits decompose into lower trigram (L1-L3) × upper trigram (L4-L6) — an 8×8 grid where each cell is a specific inner-outer situational interaction. This decomposition is different from the mirror-pair decomposition (L1↔L6, L2↔L5, L3↔L4) used throughout the algebraic investigation. Iter7 mapped their relationship.

### The maximal non-alignment

The two decompositions of Z₂⁶ are **maximally non-aligned** at the trigram boundary (L3|L4). Every mirror pair has one member on each side. Every trigram is entirely on one side. This is the structural root.

**The cross-trigram theorem**: every non-identity mirror-pair generator changes bits in both trigrams. No KW pair can share a trigram. Pairs are always full situational inversions — different inner AND outer.

### The trigram boundary theorem

An exact geometric characterization: **a bridge can preserve a trigram if and only if (1) its kernel dressing is identity (no palindromic component) and (2) all its XOR 1-bits fall on one side of the trigram boundary.**

This is a two-level gate at the L3|L4 membrane:
- Level 1 (kernel = id): no symmetric straddling
- Level 2 (one-sided XOR): no asymmetric straddling

It classifies all 31 bridges: 9 preserving, 6 near-preserving, 16 both-change.

### Three translation points

Where the two frames say the same thing in different vocabulary:

1. **Kernel silence = preservation permission.** "No palindromic component" (algebra) IS "permission to preserve a trigram" (meaning). One condition, two names.

2. **M-component = nuclear trigram rule.** L2 − L5 IS the yang count difference between nuclear trigrams. M's unique symmetric action on the ruler line is WHY this component carries meaning most transparently. Not analogy — identity.

3. **Complementary coverage = two continuity modes.** The abstract pattern from iter6 now has a concrete mechanism (see below).

### Two continuity modes

The complementary coverage from iter6 is explained as mechanism:

| Mode | Scale | Degree of freedom | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preserving bridges | Local (pair→pair) | Orientation | Trade-off pairs (67%) |
| Corridors | Periodic (every 2nd pair) | Pair ordering | KW-dom pairs (46%) |

Zero overlap. Each provides trigram continuity where the other frame is weakest. They operate through genuinely independent degrees of freedom — randomizing one preserves the other's signal.

### Corridors and the lag-4 periodicity

The lag-4 periodicity (p = 0.0017) creates **corridors** — extended regions of trigram continuity. Earth corridor through the Upper Canon core (P4-P10), Heaven corridor through the opening (P1-P5). These are a property of **pair ordering** — the first identified structural feature of the 31 ordering choices, distinct from all orientation-level findings.

### The non-separation thesis

The sage's central contribution: the trigram layer is not "between" algebra and meaning. The two decompositions were never separate. They are two ways of reading one object — six lines, indivisible, cut differently. There is no third thing connecting them. The constraint is constitutive, not imposed.

## Iter8: Pair Ordering

### The remaining degree of freedom

Orientation is fully characterized from both sides (algebraic and semantic). Pair ordering had structural characterization (corridors, lag-4) but no meaning-level account. The Xugua (序卦傳) — the traditional Commentary on the Sequence — addresses pair-to-pair transitions directly. It was unexploited data.

### Pair ordering is not a coherent design object

It is the joint trace of two independent principles — one structural, one narrative — neither of which sees pairs as units:

**1. Corridor structure** (~29 bits, ~25% of ordering freedom): trigram-persistent blocks at lag-2 spacing. Thirteen pairs in five corridors, three of which are full semantic units — the persistent trigram names the domain (Earth = collective governance, Heaven = creative limitation, Lake/Wind = relational essence).

**2. Causal narrative** (~57 bits, ~50% of ordering freedom): hex-to-hex situational chains readable in the Xugua. 23/31 transitions Direct, 8 Implied, 0 Thin. Sole ordering principle in the corridor-free zone (P17–P26), where the arrangement actively avoids trigram continuity (97th percentile Hamming distance) in favor of narrative coherence.

### Two regimes

**Corridor-rich** (P1–P16, P27–P29): structure and narrative cooperate. Corridors carry thematic arcs; the Xugua narrates smoothly between them. Overdetermined — two principles point at the same arrangement.

**Corridor-free** (P17–P26): narrative alone. The sequence actively repudiates structural continuity. T19 (Kui → Jian) hits maximum possible trigram discontinuity with a Direct causal rationale. Singly determined — narrative carries the full load.

### The Xugua as re-derivation

The Xugua is not explaining the ordering and not co-constructing it. It is **re-deriving** it — a later reader who understood the causal logic recovering the design with ~74% Direct fidelity, struggling at structural seams where the trigram architecture shifts beneath the narrative surface (6/8 Implied concentrate at corridor boundaries).

### What was rejected

**Developmental block ordering**: 0.93% of valid corridor placements have blocks in proposed developmental order vs 0.83% expected. Ratio 1.12×. Not a constraint.

## The Complete Picture

### What is established

**Orientation (32 bits)**: formally closed. Algebraic account (Pareto-optimal on 4 axes, all 27 free bits committed) and semantic account (32/32 developmental priority) jointly cover all bits with complementary coverage and zero residual. The trigram boundary theorem provides the geometric mechanism. The two accounts are not separate constraints but two readings of one object through the membrane at L3|L4.

**Pair ordering (31 choices, ~118 bits)**: regime-partitioned. Corridor structure (~25%) and causal narrative (~50%) identified. They cooperate in the corridor-rich zone and separate in the corridor-free zone, where narrative actively repudiates structure. ~25% underdetermined (corridor block placement, non-corridor pair placement).

**Matching**: fully algebraic, fully determined. Mask = signature identity.

### What is open

- Corridor block placement (~22 bits) — developmental ordering ≈ chance
- The non-corridor pairs in the corridor-rich zone (P2, P7, P12, P13, P15, P28) — attributed to narrative but not formally tested
- Whether alternative corridor-free orderings produce comparable narrative — inherently interpretive
- The nuclear trigram path — noted but unexploited
- Whether causal narrative can be formalized, or whether this boundary is final

## What Kind of Object Is the King Wen Sequence?

Synthesis-0 asked whether the sequence is an algebraic object with incidental meaning or a meaningful text with incidental mathematical properties. Eight iterations answer: **neither**.

The sequence is a jointly constrained structure where algebraic and semantic properties are two readings of one object. The constraint is not imposed from outside — it is constitutive. The six lines of a hexagram, read as mirror pairs, yield algebraic structure. The same six lines, read as trigrams, yield situational meaning. The two readings are maximally non-aligned at the trigram boundary L3|L4, which is why they cover different territory with inverse strength.

The orientation bits carry both algebraic load (Pareto optimality on four axes) and semantic load (developmental priority across 32 pairs). These are not different bits — they are the same bits, visible from different angles. Where one reading constrains tightly, the other is loose. Together they leave no bit unanchored.

The pair ordering carries structural load (corridors providing trigram scaffolding) and narrative load (causal chains providing situational coherence). These operate in two regimes — cooperating where both are present, with narrative taking full responsibility where structure withdraws.

The construction process consistent with all findings: an attentive practice operating on the full object simultaneously — tracking developmental priority (which situation creates the conditions for which), maintaining sensitivity to transitional flow (kernel anti-repetition), and producing algebraic properties as consequences of meaningful arrangement. The arranger was not computing Z₂⁶ decompositions. They were arranging situations. The algebra is what meaningful arrangement looks like when you strip the meaning away and examine the bit patterns.

The investigation has characterized the sequence from both sides and identified the geometric mechanism (the trigram boundary membrane) where the two sides meet. The remaining open questions are either underdetermined (corridor block placement) or at the boundary between the computable and the readable (whether causal narrative can be formalized). This may be the natural boundary of the investigation — not because structure runs out, but because the tools change.
