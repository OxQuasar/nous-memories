## Interpretation

### Probe 8: 體/用 × valence

The 體/用 five-phase relation significantly predicts **吉** marker distribution across the 384 states.

The 梅花 structural lens (which trigram is 體 vs 用, and their five-phase relation)
provides a detectable signal in the textual tradition. However, the signal is
**weak** (best p=0.0478) compared to the basin×凶 result (p=0.0002).

Key observation: **生体** shows the highest 吉 rate (0.444) — nearly double the
比和 rate (0.226). The 體 being nourished by 用 correlates with auspicious outcomes.
凶 trends higher in 比和 (0.202) than 生体 (0.069) — the opposite of naive prediction.
But the 比和 vs 克 focused tests are NOT significant (Fisher p>0.37 for both 吉 and 凶),
so the 5-category pattern is driven by 生体's elevated 吉, not by a 比和/克 axis.

### Probe 7: Depth boundary and 凶 content

No word group reaches significance at α=0.05.
Marginal trends: threshold (p<0.10).

**Threshold words** (終, 已, 極, 窮, 盡) appear in 2/14 depth-1 凶 texts but
0/38 other 凶 texts (Fisher p=0.069). This is suggestive but not conclusive —
the sample size (52 total 凶 texts) limits statistical power.

**Qualitative observation**: The depth-1 凶 texts are dominated by two hexagrams:
剝 (KW23, Splitting Apart — 3 凶 lines) and 頤 (KW27, Nourishment — 3 凶 lines),
both in the Kun basin. The Qian basin depth-1 凶 texts include 大過 (Great Exceeding)
and 夬/姤 (Breakthrough/Coming to Meet). The thematic pattern is structural
extremity — peeling away, excess, breakthrough — but this is circumstantial,
not statistically confirmed.

The 凶×basin signal (from Probe 1) is **distributional** — WHERE 凶 appears —
not thematic — what the 凶 texts SAY. The content of 凶 texts does not
systematically differentiate by depth category at current sample sizes.

### Probe 6: KW sequence structure

**Strong result**: Consecutive hexagrams stay within the same basin MORE than chance
(38/63 = 60.3%, p=0.0000). The King Wen sequence has algebraic locality.

However, this is **entirely driven by within-pair structure**: all 32 within-pair
transitions are KW partners (reversal/complement), which tend to preserve basin.
Between-pair transitions show NO basin locality (p=0.7619).

**Depth transitions** are overwhelmingly Δ=0 (51/63 = 81%), meaning consecutive
hexagrams usually share the same convergence depth. This is partly explained by
the 4:12:48 depth distribution — most hexagrams are depth-2, so random pairs
would also show high same-depth rates.

**Five-phase relations** between consecutive hexagrams show NO elevated 比和 rate
(upper p=0.87, lower p=0.79). The KW sequence does not favor element-same
transitions — it is element-neutral in its ordering.
