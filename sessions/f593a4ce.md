**Complement symmetry** — what it says:

Take S0 (000) = all trends down, and S7 (111) = all trends up. Their transition rows in J₈ are nearly identical after you swap each destination with its complement. Why? Because the mechanism driving a fast-timescale trend divergence from slower trends is direction-agnostic. A 1h trend flipping positive while 8h and 48h remain negative has the same dynamics as 1h flipping negative while 8h and 48h remain positive. The coupling between oscillators depends on their phase relationship (aligned vs misaligned), not their absolute polarity.

This is non-obvious. It would BREAK if:
- Crashes are faster than rallies (asymmetric volatility)
- There's a structural drift/bias at these timescales
- Liquidity is directionally asymmetric

Three oscillators at different frequencies (fast=1h, medium=8h, slow=48h). Each alternates positive/negative. The regime is determined by their alignment pattern:
