## The Three Layers

1. **Algebra (skeleton):** 10.8–11.0% of variance. Static contribution: grouping/labeling via partition. The *dynamics* (生/克 cycles) that make the algebra mathematically special are absent from the texts (R192). Model-invariant.
2. **Text (flesh):** ~89% residual. ~16-dimensional thematic manifold (cross-model consensus). Design principles: near-neighbor differentiation (R159, cross-model) + complement antipodality (R156). Holistic, non-decomposable. Irreducibly non-algebraic.
3. **KW ordering (narrative thread):** Locally smooth (5.6th percentile), globally shapeless. Semantic, not algebraic — zero binary or torus signal (R189). Layer-stratified: tuan (99.7%) > guaci (92%) > yaoci (80%), correlating with discursive freedom (R190). Authorial confound not separable.

### The complement bridges all three
- Algebraically forced (Tier 1)
- Textually manifest in ~16 independent directions (Tier 1b)
- KW ordering respects it (complement proximity)

## East/West Investigation (R181–R196)

### Central Results

1. **φ and 五行 dual cycles share Q(ζ₅) as address, not cause** (R183–R188). The cyclotomic field is where both structures live, but rigidity is arithmetic ((p−3)/2)!=1, 2^{n−1}=n+1), not field-theoretic. φ tags Shape A (three-type coexistence) at p=5 specifically.

2. **n=3 is the dimension threshold for relational richness** (R181–R182). At n=2, the only viable prime is p=3, where stride-1 ≡ stride-(−1) — a single Hamiltonian cycle. Dual-cycle dynamics require n≥3. This constrains the conjunction: binary encoding + dual cycles requires n≥3. It does NOT foreclose five-element systems without binary substrates.

3. **The texts use the five-phase partition, not the five-phase dynamics** (R192). 生/克 relations carry no semantic information. The ~11% algebraic variance operates through grouping, not through relational cycles. The feature that makes 五行 mathematically special is exactly the feature absent from the texts.

4. **KW ordering is semantic, not algebraic** (R189–R190). Zero binary or torus signal; significant text-embedding smoothness with authorial stratification.

5. **No universal differentiation principle** (R196). R191 (surface cell anti-signal) retracted after failing cross-model replication. R159 (Hamming V-shape) unaffected — the retraction was of the generalization, not the original finding.

## Methodological Notes

### Vulnerability (noted, not fixable)
All text-intrinsic findings depend on multilingual transformer embedding models trained on modern web corpora. Cross-model validation (3 models, 3 labs) mitigates model-specific artifacts but cannot address shared architectural assumptions. A radically different embedding approach (classical-Chinese-only, non-neural) could in principle yield different results. The tier system accounts for this: Tier 1b = "text-intrinsic as measured by current best methods."

### Infrastructure
- Embeddings: `synthesis/embeddings.npz` (BGE-M3, 384 yaoci × 1024 dim)
- Cross-model embeddings: `Q1/embeddings_{bge-m3,e5-large,labse}.npz`
- Atlas: `atlas/atlas.json`
- Residual extraction: `Q1/phase1_residual_structure.py`
- Climate data: `T3/climate_normals_cache.json` (97 cities, 30-year normals)
- GPU: Quadro P5000, 16GB. sentence-transformers + FlagEmbedding in venv.

### Research Program Dynamics
- R191 retraction demonstrates healthy methodology: proposed generalization → systematic test → failure → retraction. Claims get tested, overgeneralizations get caught.
- Authorial confound (tuan > guaci > yaoci pattern) recurs across multiple analyses. Layer-stratified signals require skepticism — correlation with discursive freedom, not just dating.
- The "numerically positive, structurally negative" distinction (T3) is reusable: cardinality match ≠ structural match. Similarly at the cyclotomic level: field containment ≠ causal connection.
- Cross-model validation standard: ρ > 0.78 for Tier 1b. Single-model 92nd percentile (p ≈ 0.08) does not meet bar.
