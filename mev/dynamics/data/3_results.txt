KEY FINDINGS
============

1. TEMPORAL CONCENTRATION — Liquidations are extremely spikey:
   - In ALL crash epochs, 3 days account for 45-89% of total volume.
   - 2024_consolidation: a SINGLE day (Aug 5 2024) produced $187M = 61.9% of epoch volume.
   - 2026_crash: 3 days (Jan 31, Feb 5-6) produced 86.6% of volume.
   - Spike days cluster around -8% to -18% daily price moves.

2. LIQUIDATION INTENSITY IS ROUGHLY LINEAR (elasticity = 0.83):
   - Log-log regression: volume ~ |price_drop|^0.83
   - Not strongly convex or concave — no clear acceleration in larger crashes.
   - BUT intensity per 1% varies enormously across epochs:
     2022_bear_2: $29M/1% (lowest — FTX crash, markets already deleveraged)
     2024_consolidation: $774M/1% (highest — yen carry unwind, heavily leveraged)
     This 26× variation suggests leverage buildup matters more than crash magnitude.

3. STRUCTURAL SHIFT IN LEVERAGE:
   - 2022_bear_2 ($12M total, -42%) vs 2024_consolidation ($302M, -39%):
     Similar % drops but 24× more liquidation volume in 2024.
   - The system carries far more leverage in 2024-2026 than 2022.
   - Intensity floor has risen: post-2024, even mild crashes trigger >$190M.

4. COLLATERAL COMPOSITION SHIFT:
   - 2022: WETH was 92-98% of real liquidation collateral (stETH just 2-8%)
   - 2024_consolidation: wstETH surged to 32.8% of collateral (WETH dropped to 64%)
   - 2025_q4_chop: wstETH became MAJORITY at 55.9% of real liquidation collateral
   - 2026_crash: wstETH at 20.8%
   This confirms the hypothesis: as LST adoption grew, "real" positions increasingly
   use wstETH as collateral instead of WETH. The collateral is shifting from raw ETH
   to yield-bearing ETH derivatives.

5. 2025_CRASH had highest event count (8,262) and most users liquidated (4,162).
   The -61% drawdown created a broad liquidation cascade.
   First >$10M day came 49 days into the crash — slow bleed then spike.

6. SPIKE TIMING PATTERN:
   - 2022_bear_1: first >$10M day at day 20 (Jan 21, crypto winter onset)
   - 2024_consolidation: first >$10M day at day 8 (rapid yen carry unwind)
   - 2025_crash: first >$10M day at day 49 (slow bleed, then Feb 3 waterfall)
   - 2026_crash: first >$10M day at day 45 (late-stage capitulation)
   Slow-burn crashes have delayed spike onset; sudden deleveraging events spike early.

7. POST-SPIKE RECOVERY EPOCHS show minimal liquidation activity:
   - 2022_rally: $6.3M total (negligible after $229M crash)
   - 2025_recovery: $22.3M total (after $315M crash)
   - 2026_recovery: $0.95M total (after $193M crash)
   Recoveries have ~95% less liquidation volume than crashes — asymmetric.
