KEY FINDINGS
============

1. RECHARGE MODEL — WEAK CORRELATION (r = 0.29):
   Simple pre-crash borrowed_start (USD) vs total real liquidation volume: r = 0.289.
   Pre-crash utilization is slightly better: r = 0.585 but n=6 is too small for significance.
   Pre-crash leverage level alone does NOT predict liquidation volume.
   
   Why it fails:
   - 2025_q4_chop had $24.1B borrowed (highest) but only $217M liquidated
   - 2022_bear_1 had $6.0B borrowed (3rd lowest) but $229M liquidated
   - The crash MAGNITUDE (how far price drops) matters as much as leverage level
   - It's an interaction: leverage × crash_depth → liquidation volume

2. AAVE GROWTH — PROTOCOL SCALED 4× IN 4 YEARS:
   - Jan 2022: $8.5B TVL, $6.0B borrowed (v2 only)
   - Mar 2026: $20.4B TVL, $13.3B borrowed (mostly v3)
   - Peak: $32.8B TVL, $24.4B borrowed (Aug 2025, before Q4 chop)
   - Borrowed in ETH terms: 1.6M ETH (2022) → 6.1M ETH (2026) = 3.8× real growth

3. UTILIZATION DYNAMICS — TWO DISTINCT PATTERNS:
   
   a) Classic crashes (2022_bear_1, 2025_crash, 2025_q4_chop):
      Utilization DROPS (-2 to -10pp). Borrowing falls faster than deposits.
      Borrowers deleverage; depositors are stickier. This is healthy deleveraging.
   
   b) Anomalous crashes (2022_bear_2, 2024_consolidation):
      Utilization RISES (+4 to +6pp). Deposits flee faster than borrowers.
      2024_consolidation: borrowing actually INCREASED (+15.3%) during -39% crash!
      This suggests forced refinancing or reluctance to close positions.

4. 2024_CONSOLIDATION IS AN OUTLIER:
   - Only crash where borrowing increased (+15.3%)
   - Highest liquidation intensity ($774M per 1% of price decline)
   - Single-day concentration: Aug 5 produced 62% of epoch volume
   - Yen carry unwind was external shock hitting maximally leveraged positions

5. DELEVERAGING ASYMMETRY:
   - Bull recoveries add leverage fast: 2025_recovery +177% borrowed in 136 days
   - Crashes remove leverage slower: 2025_crash -34% borrowed in 113 days
   - Leverage rebuilds faster than it unwinds (contradicts the "fast deleverage" hypothesis)
   - Exception: 2022_bear_1 deleveraged -69% (truly wiped the system)

6. UTILIZATION EQUILIBRIUM:
   - System gravitates toward 38-42% utilization
   - Crashes pull it down slightly, recoveries push it back up
   - The 2022_bear_1 was the only event that broke below 30%
   - Post-2024, utilization stays remarkably stable even through crashes
   - Avg crash Δutil = -0.9pp, avg bull Δutil = +1.1pp — nearly symmetric
