## Task 2: Correlation Analysis

### Output Files
- `data/correlation_matrix.csv` — 15×15 full-period Pearson matrix
- `data/rolling_correlations.csv` — 1538 rows, 10 rolling correlation series
- Script: `memories/mev/links/correlations.py`

### Key Findings

**Analysis 1 — Full-Period Correlations:**
- ETH return's strongest correlator: BTC (r=+0.82), then ETH/BTC ratio (r=+0.69), then S&P 500 (r=+0.43)
- VIX change is anti-correlated with ETH (r=-0.36) — fear hurts ETH
- Liquidation count negatively correlates with ETH return (r=-0.22) — expected
- DXY weakly inverse (r=-0.13), gold near zero (r=+0.06)
- ETH/BTC return: S&P (r=+0.23), VIX change (r=-0.20), liq_count (r=-0.14) — ETH underperforms BTC when liquidations spike

**Analysis 2 — Rolling Correlations:**
- ETH/BTC: extremely stable, mean 0.82, never below 0.34
- ETH/S&P: mean 0.38 but ranges from -0.38 to +0.84 — regime-dependent
- ETH/liq_count: mean -0.26, spikes to -0.91 during crash cascades
- ETH/DXY: mean -0.14, unstable (range -0.67 to +0.55)

**Analysis 3 — Crash vs Calm:**
- ETH/S&P correlation rises from +0.38 (calm) to +0.49 (crash) — correlation convergence in stress
- ETH/BTC rises from +0.79 to +0.88 — everything moves together in crashes
- ETH/liq_count: -0.14 calm → -0.29 crash — liquidations double their correlation impact during crashes
- ETH/BTC vs total_borrowed flips: +0.04 calm → -0.08 crash — when borrowed is high, ETH underperforms BTC more during crashes

**Analysis 4 — Lead/Lag:**
- ALL variables peak at lag=0 — no daily lead/lag detected at ±5 days
- Liquidation count shows persistent negative correlation at lags 1-5 (r=-0.22 to -0.07), suggesting liquidation cascades extend over multiple days
- S&P/BTC/VIX/DXY: all contemporaneous, no detectable daily lead structure

**Analysis 5 — Calm ETH/BTC vs Borrow Growth:**
- Near-zero correlation in all regimes (Spearman r≈0.00, p≈0.81-0.99)
- No change pre-2024 vs post-2024 (BTC ETF had no effect on this relationship)
- Lagged tests also null — borrow growth does NOT explain calm-period ETH/BTC decline
- Implication: ETH/BTC decline is structural/narrative, not leverage-driven
