Step 1b Results: Wall Decomposition — Real vs Phantom
======================================================
Date: 2026-03-20
ETH reference price: $2,202.39

Methodology
-----------
Positions classified by debt composition:
  - Real:    ETH debt fraction < 20% (stablecoin debt → ETH price drop triggers liquidation)
  - Phantom: ETH debt fraction > 80% (ETH debt + ETH collateral → both sides move together)
  - Mixed:   20-80% ETH debt

When a user borrows ETH against ETH collateral, a drop in ETH price reduces
both the collateral value AND the debt value proportionally. The health factor
stays approximately constant. These positions appear in naive liquidation maps
but are NOT triggered by ETH price drops — only by depeg events between
ETH-correlated assets (e.g., wstETH/ETH ratio breaking).

Implications for Thesis
-----------------------
1. The naive liquidation heatmap drastically overstates near-price liquidation risk.
   What looks like a $3.8B wall at -5% is actually <$1M of real exposure.

2. Real liquidation walls are much further from current price (30-50% below).
   This means escalation from moderate stress episodes (5-15% drops) is unlikely
   to trigger large cascade liquidations on Aave v3 at current position topology.

3. Phantom positions DO create depeg cascade risk: if wstETH/ETH or weETH/ETH
   breaks (e.g., during extreme market stress or a protocol exploit), the $5.6B
   in phantom positions would cascade. But this is a different risk factor than
   ETH price movement.

4. For historical backtesting (Step 3), position type classification is essential.
   Historical "walls" must be decomposed the same way — phantom walls in 2022-2023
   may have been different (LSTs were less dominant, more plain ETH collateral).
