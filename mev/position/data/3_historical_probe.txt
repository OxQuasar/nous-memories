Approach Used
-------------
1. Get v2 reserve list and debt token addresses via on-chain calls at block 14935369
2. Find all borrowers via alchemy_getAssetTransfers (scan debt token mints from
   zero address across all 68 debt tokens in a single batched scan)
3. Multicall getUserAccountData at historical block for all 33,794 borrower candidates
4. getUserReserveData per-user per-reserve for 21 key reserves (covers >99% of TVL)
5. Classify positions by debt composition (real/phantom/mixed)

This works because Alchemy free tier supports archive eth_call at any historical
block (though eth_getLogs is limited to 10-block ranges).

Thesis Validation
-----------------
This snapshot provides strong evidence for the wall proximity thesis:

✅ There WAS a significant real wall in June 2022 ($282M at $1,185, plus $317M
   total in the $1,150-$1,200 bin) — 34% below the starting price.

✅ That wall WAS breached during the crash, contributing to cascading liquidations.

✅ The 2026 snapshot shows no comparable wall within 30% — consistent with
   the hypothesis that wall proximity predicts escalation risk.

The comparison suggests:
- June 2022 topology: dense real walls at 16-40% below → crash escalated through them
- March 2026 topology: thin gradient, first real concentration at 30%+ below → 
  a similar 20% drop today would hit far less real liquidation volume

This does NOT prove causation, but the structural difference is stark.
