======================================================================
SUMMARY & INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

1. ETF OUTFLOWS CONSISTENTLY PRECEDE AND ACCOMPANY SPIKE DAYS
   - Average BTC ETF flow on day -1 before spikes: -$304M. Day 0: -$225M.
   - 12/14 spike days with ETF data show negative BTC flows in the [-1, 0]
     window. The two exceptions (2025-02-03, 2025-10-10) are "isolated"
     spike days that came during net-positive flow periods.
   - Cluster-initiating days show the strongest signal: day -1 flow
     averages -$595M — nearly 2x the overall average.

2. OUTFLOWS PERSIST DURING MULTI-DAY CLUSTERS
   - 2025_q4_chop cluster (Nov 16→20→21): cumulative BTC outflows of
     -$621M (pre), -$552M (during), and continued negative into terminal.
   - 2026_crash cluster (Jan 31→Feb 5→6): -$985M pre-initiation,
     continued -$434M mid-cluster, reversal to +$371M at terminal.
   - The terminal day of each cluster shows flow REVERSAL (positive flows
     on day-of or post-3d) — consistent with "capitulation bottom" pattern.

3. ETH ETF FLOWS AMPLIFY THE SIGNAL
   - 2025-11-04: -$418M ETH ETF pre-3d + -$219M day-of. This is an isolated
     day with severe return (-8.6%) — ETH-specific outflow pressure.
   - Nov 16-21 cluster: cumulative ETH outflows -$550M pre, -$178M day-of,
     tapering through the cluster. ETH ETF selling compounds BTC-driven
     crypto risk-off.

4. JANUARY 2025 MYSTERY: RESOLVED
   - Jan 2025 BTC ETF: +$5,256M net INFLOW. ETH ETF: +$102M net.
   - This was NOT an ETF outflow period — flows were strongly positive.
   - Week of Jan 5-12: ETH ETF saw -$187M outflows while BTC got +$313M
     — a brief BTC-dominance rotation that may partially explain the
     ETH-specific weakness.
   - But the -11.2% ETH residual with only 18 liqs/day is NOT explained
     by ETF outflows. The drawdown likely reflects CEX/perp-driven selling
     or macro repositioning (non-DeFi, non-ETF channels).

5. PREDICTIVE POWER: WEAK (N TOO SMALL)
   - Pre-3d BTC flow vs liquidation count: ρ=+0.30, p=0.30 — directionally
     correct (more outflows → fewer events? counterintuitive) but not significant.
   - Pre-3d BTC flow vs cluster continuation: p=0.55 — no predictive power.
   - N=14 spike days is too small for robust statistical inference. The
     patterns are visible in the event study but cannot be confirmed.

6. KEY PATTERN: OUTFLOW → SPIKE → REVERSAL
   The consistent pattern across clusters is:
   (a) BTC ETF outflows begin 1-3 days before the first spike day
   (b) Outflows persist or intensify through the cluster
   (c) Terminal spike day shows flow reversal (inflows begin)
   (d) This matches the "capitulation" thesis: ETF outflows represent
       institutional selling pressure that compounds with DeFi liquidations,
       and the cascade ends when selling pressure exhausts.

7. CAUSAL DIRECTION REMAINS AMBIGUOUS
   ETF outflows could be:
   (a) CAUSAL: institutional selling → price decline → DeFi liquidations
   (b) REACTIVE: price decline → panicked redemptions → additional selling
   (c) BOTH: negative feedback loop between ETF flows and DeFi mechanics
   
   The fact that outflows BEGIN 1-3 days before spike days leans toward (a)
   or (c) — the institutional selling pressure appears to initiate, not
   just follow, the liquidation cascade. But with N=14, this could be
   coincidence.

8. IMPLICATIONS FOR PHYSICS FRAMEWORK
   - ETF flows are a "slow physical process" (T+1 settlement, institutional
     decision latency) that creates predictable selling pressure.
   - The 5-7 day cascade timescale (dynamics phase) may partially reflect
     the duration of institutional redemption waves — once a drawdown starts,
     ETF outflows persist for 3-5 trading days, providing continued price
     pressure that keeps triggering DeFi liquidation batches.
   - Monitoring ETF flow data (published daily after market close) could
     provide early warning of cascade continuation vs termination.
