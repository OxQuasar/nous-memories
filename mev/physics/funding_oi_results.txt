======================================================================
SUMMARY & INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

1. DIRECTIONAL SIGNAL EXISTS BUT IS WEAK STATISTICALLY
   - After negative funding, OI drops -0.37% in 0-4h vs +0.04% after positive funding.
   - Welch t-test is marginal (p=0.045), Mann-Whitney borderline (p=0.053).
   - The deeply negative bin (n=11) shows a large effect (-1.35%) but sample is tiny
     and not significant vs deeply positive (p=0.27).

2. FRONT-LOADING PATTERN IN DEEPLY NEGATIVE
   - After deeply negative funding: 0-4h mean=-1.35%, 4-8h mean=+0.08%.
   - The closure is concentrated in the first 4h window, with mild reversal after.
   - However p=0.16 — not significant given n=11. Suggestive but not conclusive.

3. NO PREEMPTIVE CLOSING
   - Pre-settlement OI change for deeply negative: +0.44% (OI was INCREASING
     before settlement). Traders are not closing ahead of negative funding charges.

4. PRICE DOMINATES, FUNDING ADDS LITTLE
   - Price-conditional analysis: negative funding + price falling (-0.47%) vs
     positive funding + price falling (-0.28%) → p=0.34, not significant.
   - Within negative funding, price falling vs rising: p=0.19, not significant.
   - Price direction explains most of the OI change; funding sign adds minimal
     additional predictive power.

5. SURPRISING: DEEPLY POSITIVE FUNDING ALSO SHOWS OI DROPS
   - > 3bps funding: 0-4h OI change = -0.57%. This is OPPOSITE to what the
     hypothesis predicts. High positive funding should not cause OI closure.
   - Interpretation: deeply positive funding often occurs during extreme rallies
     followed by mean-reversion. The OI drop likely reflects profit-taking / 
     forced unwinding of overleveraged longs, not a funding-rate-driven effect.
   - This suggests OI changes are driven by price dynamics, not funding mechanics.

CONCLUSION:
The hypothesis — that negative funding settlements cause clustered OI closure in
0-4h — has a directional signal but is NOT statistically robust. The effect is:
  (a) Marginal significance in broad negative vs positive comparison
  (b) Not significant in the extreme tails (n too small)
  (c) Not significant after conditioning on price direction
  (d) Confounded by the fact that deeply positive funding shows similar OI drops

The dominant driver of OI changes is price movement, not funding settlement timing.
Negative funding is a lagging indicator of conditions that also cause OI drops,
not a causal driver of them.
