Step 1 Results: DEX Liquidity Depth for LST Tokens
====================================================
Date: 2026-03-20 | ETH price: $2,136

METHOD
------
Used Paraswap aggregator API to simulate sells of increasing sizes for each LST→WETH.
Paraswap routes through ALL available DEX liquidity (Uniswap V3, Curve, Balancer, 
SushiSwap, etc.) simultaneously, giving real-world aggregated depth. Adaptive bisection
refinement used to find the exact liquidity cliff for each token.

KEY FINDINGS
------------

1. EXTREME LIQUIDITY-EXPOSURE MISMATCH
   Every LST has a cascade ratio >20x — the phantom exposure behind each token's
   depeg threshold massively exceeds the selling needed to trigger it.
   
   Worst cases:
   - ETHx: $100M of phantom exposure, $21K of DEX liquidity (4,681x)
   - osETH: $332M phantom, <$1M DEX capacity (856x)
   - cbETH: $150M phantom, $390K capacity (583x)
   - rsETH: $880M phantom, $2M capacity — pool EXHAUSTS before reaching 1% depeg (434x)

2. LIQUIDITY CLIFF STRUCTURE — NOT GRADUAL
   Most tokens show a sharp cliff where the aggregator cannot find any route:
   - weETH: smooth up to 5000 ETH (0.5% depeg), then jumps to 13.4% at 7500 ETH
   - wstETH: smooth up to 2000 ETH (0.09% depeg), then jumps to 7.6% at 2750 ETH
   - rsETH: smooth up to 950 ETH (0.44% depeg), then NO ROUTE at 969 ETH
   - osETH: smooth up to 100 ETH (0.12% depeg), then 9.3% at 500 ETH
   
   This means DEX liquidity is concentrated in a narrow band. Once exhausted,
   there is effectively NO remaining depth — price falls off a cliff.

3. TIER 3 TOKENS ARE HAIR-TRIGGER CANARIES
   - osETH: $388K of selling produces 2% depeg. $332M of phantom positions sit behind it.
   - ETHx: $21K of selling exhausts ALL liquidity. $100M of phantom positions.
   - These tokens would crack first in any LST selling event.

4. wstETH IS NOT DEEP
   Despite being the "bluest chip" LST, wstETH only has ~$5.7M of aggregated DEX
   capacity. The Curve stETH/ETH pool (which used to hold >$1B) now has only ~$40M
   TVL (~20K ETH per side). Most wstETH liquidity has migrated to lending protocols
   (Aave, Spark), not DEXes.
   
   $4.7M of selling produces 2% depeg in wstETH. $1.7B of phantom exposure sits
   behind it. Cascade ratio: 363x.

5. weETH IS THE DEEPEST BUT STILL VULNERABLE  
   weETH has the deepest DEX liquidity (~$13.9M via Curve StableNg + Uniswap V3).
   $11.3M produces 2% depeg. But $230M of phantom positions sit at <1% depeg —
   a 20x cascade ratio. This is the most "efficient" attacker target: the largest
   phantom exposure relative to trigger cost.

6. DEX ROUTING STRUCTURE
   Small sells route through Uniswap V3 (concentrated liquidity, tight spread).
   Larger sells add Curve pools. At the limit, the router adds SushiSwap, UniswapV2,
   and token-specific unwrap paths (wstETH→stETH→Curve). This shows the liquidity
   is fragmented — no single deep pool.

IMPLICATION FOR CASCADE MECHANICS
----------------------------------

The cascade vulnerability is confirmed and quantified:

A. TRIGGER COST: As little as $21K (ETHx) to $11.3M (weETH) to produce meaningful depeg
B. ACTIVATION: Depeg triggers phantom position liquidations (HF drops below 1.0)
C. AMPLIFICATION: Liquidations sell more LST collateral, deepening the depeg
D. The cascade ratio (20x-4,681x) means the activated selling pressure vastly
   exceeds the liquidity that absorbed the original trigger

The self-sustaining cascade threshold: once the initial selling exhausts DEX
liquidity (the cliff), liquidation selling has ZERO remaining depth to absorb it.
Every subsequent liquidation directly moves the price toward zero.

CAVEATS
-------
- Paraswap refuses quotes beyond ~33% price impact ("NO ROUTE"), so capacity may be
  slightly higher than reported but with catastrophic slippage
- These are instantaneous liquidity snapshots; real cascades unfold over minutes/hours
  allowing JIT liquidity and arbitrageurs — but also allowing MORE panic selling
- Phantom exposure numbers are from the position topology investigation; actual
  exposure may shift as users adjust positions
