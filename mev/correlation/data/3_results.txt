IMPLICATIONS FOR CASCADE SPEED
-------------------------------
During a real stETH DEX depeg:
1. DEX price moves instantly (seconds)
2. Chainlink feed DOES NOT UPDATE for up to 24 hours
3. Aave oracle reads stale Chainlink price → HF remains artificially high
4. No liquidations occur until the next heartbeat update

But: if the deviation threshold is 0.5%, a genuine >0.5% stETH depeg on
the Chainlink oracle network (not DEX) would trigger an early update. 
However, Chainlink oracles aggregate reports from multiple node operators
who may source prices from different DEXes — so there could be a 
multi-minute lag between DEX depeg and oracle update.

Worst case: if a depeg occurs right AFTER a heartbeat update, the oracle
won't reflect it for ~24 hours. This creates a window where:
- Positions SHOULD be liquidated but aren't
- The depeg can deepen on DEX without triggering Aave liquidations
- When the oracle finally updates, the depeg is worse → larger cascade

Best case: depeg triggers deviation-threshold update within minutes. But
this has never happened in recent history (no deviation updates in 20 rounds).

The 24h heartbeat means the cascade loop speed is NOT governed by the
Chainlink feed — instead, the cascade would be a delayed-onset event
where DEX depeg accumulates for hours before Aave oracle catches up.

All three implied market feeds are within 0.4% of 1.0.

FEED STRUCTURES
---------------
1. wstETH: Uses a genuine Chainlink stETH/ETH feed (confirmed at
   0x86392dC19c..., returning ~0.9999). The adapter computes:
   price = cap(stEthPerToken()) × stETH/ETH_chainlink × ETH/USD

   This means a genuine stETH depeg DOES flow through to wstETH pricing,
   but only at the Chainlink feed's update frequency (24h heartbeat).

2. weETH: Uses getRate() from the weETH contract (protocol-level rate).
   The implied market feed is ~1.002, suggesting either:
   (a) There IS a small eETH/ETH Chainlink feed very close to 1.0, or
   (b) Direct ratio × ETH/USD with rounding/timing noise

   Since eETH/ETH DEX liquidity is essentially zero ($77K), if there IS
   an eETH/ETH Chainlink feed, it would be sourcing prices from an
   extremely thin market.

3. osETH: Uses convertToAssets(1e18) on the StakeWise vault (ERC4626 pattern).
   Implied feed ~1.002. Same structure as weETH — likely ratio × ETH/USD.

KEY FINDING: wstETH is the ONLY LST where a genuine DEX market feed
(stETH/ETH Chainlink) sits in the oracle path. For weETH and osETH,
the oracle reads protocol-level exchange rates, NOT market prices.

IMPLICATIONS
------------
This creates an asymmetry in cascade vulnerability:

- wstETH: A stETH DEX depeg → Chainlink stETH/ETH updates (with 24h lag) →
  Aave oracle reflects depeg → liquidations trigger. BUT: the 24h lag means
  the cascade has a delay fuse.

- weETH/osETH: A DEX depeg DOES NOT directly affect the Aave oracle because
  the oracle reads protocol rates, not market prices. Liquidations would only
  trigger if:
  (a) The protocol rate itself changes (slashing event), or
  (b) A CAPO misconfiguration occurs (like the March 10 incident), or
  (c) ETH/USD drops (standard liquidation, not phantom-specific)

  This means the weETH/osETH cascade from Step 1 (DEX depth → depeg → liquidation)
  may NOT actually work through the oracle path in the simple way we assumed.
  The phantom positions are protected by the protocol-rate oracle, not exposed
  to DEX market prices.

  HOWEVER: during a genuine market stress event, protocol rates CAN deviate
  from market rates (withdrawal queue delays, slashing), and arbitrageurs
  selling LSTs on DEX would create real depeg pressure that eventually
  manifests in protocol rates.


REVISED CASCADE MODEL
---------------------
Given these findings, the cascade operates differently for each LST:

wstETH cascade (delayed-onset):
  1. Trigger: sell pressure on stETH DEX ($4.5M → 1% depeg)
  2. Chainlink stETH/ETH: updates after up to 24h
  3. Aave wstETH oracle: reflects depeg with ~24h lag
  4. Liquidations: fire when oracle updates
  5. Liquidated wstETH: sold on DEX, deepening depeg
  6. Loop speed: one cycle per ~24h (slow but relentless)

weETH/osETH cascade (protocol-rate dependent):
  1. Trigger: market stress causes DEX depeg
  2. Aave oracle: reads protocol rate, does NOT immediately reflect DEX depeg
  3. Phantom positions: temporarily shielded by protocol-rate oracle
  4. BUT: if protocol rate changes (slashing, queue delay), oracle updates instantly
  5. Then: liquidations → DEX selling → deeper depeg

The March 10 CAPO incident showed that a 2.85% oracle mispricing can
trigger $27M of liquidations in 84 seconds. A genuine oracle update
reflecting real depeg would trigger the same mechanism at the same speed.
