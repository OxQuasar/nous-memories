# Investigation 1: Intraday Regime Transition Structure

## Status: OOS-VALIDATED — March 2026 (12 phases)

**Result: Clean informative negative on (3,5). K=4, not K=5. The 4-regime cycle is a universal property of multi-timescale trend coupling. Exit prediction dominated by trend_8h (medium trend proximity to zero crossing). OOS-validated on 2 years of BTC 2023-2024 ($16.7k–$108k) — topology confirmed, cross-domain AUC 0.957 (C2), 0.980 (C1).**

---

## Question

Does the empirical intraday transition structure for BTC match the (3,5) predictions? And if not, what DOES the transition structure look like?

**Answer:** No. The market has 4 functional regime types (not 5), organized as a directed cycle with forbidden stage-skipping. Complement symmetry holds (a necessary condition for (3,5)), but the sufficient conditions fail — K=4 beats K=5 by 19× in lumpability error across all 240 complement-equivariant surjections. The (3,5) hypothesis was also tested as relation-typing (5 transition types between 8 states) and rejected there too — transitions decompose into 4 types.

The K=4 cycle is universal across 6+ binary bases, 4 timescale ratios, 4 time-of-day blocks, 3 subperiods, and independently rediscovered in both halves of a temporal split. It is generated by endogenous multi-timescale feedback, invariant under time-translation, direction-reversal, and timescale rescaling.

Regime exit quality is dominated by trend_8h (medium trend) proximity to its zero crossing. Cross-validated AUC: 0.973 (C2), 0.965 (C1). The binary S5/S4 signal was a noisy proxy for this continuous predictor.

**OOS validation (Phase 12):** All structural properties independently confirmed on 2 years of BTC 2023-2024 data — a completely different macro environment ($16.7k→$108k, post-FTX recovery to ATH). Cross-domain logistic model: AUC 0.957 (C2), 0.980 (C1). C1 breakthrough returns virtually identical (+1.08% OOS vs +1.09% IS). Binary S5/S4 signal degraded in OOS (environment-dependent), superseded by continuous trend_8h model.

### Twelve phases of analysis

1. **Phase 1: Diagnostics** — Transition matrices, eigenvalues, dwell times, subperiod stability for bases A/B/C
2. **Phase 2: 4×4 characterization** — Collapsed Basis A, discovered directed cycle and C2 heterogeneity. New Basis D with complement symmetry test.
3. **Phase 3: (3,5) closure** — Exhaustive 240-surjection search. K=4 wins by 19×.
4. **Phase 4: Regime-conditioned returns** — Duration, exit destination, price returns. C2 exit signal quantified.
5. **Phase 5: Flip dynamics** — Intra-episode S4↔S5 flips. Trigger is noise; signal is exit property.
6. **Phase 6: Forward-looking returns** — Exit sub-state predicts next regime quality. Generalizes across all transitions.
7. **Phase 7: Transition-level clustering** — Z₅ relation-typing tested and rejected. Transitions decompose into 4 types (within/forward/backward/forbidden), matching K=4 macro-cycle. (3,5) definitively closed.
8. **Phase 8: Temporal modulation** — Transition TYPE is time-invariant (all p > 0.05). S5→bull signal session-independent (p = 0.567). Mild weekend effect (faster cycling, higher C1 breakthrough). Transition frequency mildly non-uniform (p = 0.038).
9. **Phase 9: Timescale universality** — K=4 cycle holds at all 4 timescale ratios. Duration scales linearly with slow timescale. Exit signal depends on fast:medium ratio (bandwidth ~1:4 to 1:8). Signal universality is regime-dependent (C1 always informative, C2 only at ratio ≤ 1:8).
10. **Phase 10: Signal refinement + split-half** — trend_1h magnitude is a continuous predictor (ρ=0.533). Duration adds nothing (Fisher p=1.00). Split-half blind re-discovery: K=4, zeros, complement symmetry independently confirmed in both halves.
11. **Phase 11: Logistic regression at exits** — trend_8h dominates exit prediction (C2 AUC 0.973, C1 AUC 0.965). Binary S5/S4 was a proxy. C1 upgraded from "mostly unpredictable" to "substantially predictable." Interaction term adds nothing (M3 ≈ M2).
12. **Phase 12: OOS validation** — Full validation on BTC 2023-2024. Topology confirmed (K=4, identical structural zeros, complement JSD 0.007). Cross-domain logistic AUC 0.957 (C2), 0.980 (C1). Binary signal degraded (environment-dependent). Returns confirmed (C1 breakthrough +1.08% vs IS +1.09%). Bit-flip attribution: 86.4% of exits driven by trend_8h.

### 6. Temporal Invariance

Transition TYPE is time-invariant (all p > 0.05). Transition TIMING mildly non-uniform (Asia 28.3% vs Evening 21.9%, p = 0.038). The market changes regimes more often during certain hours, but *which* regime it transitions to is the same regardless of when.

Weekend: faster cycling, higher breakthrough. Consistent with lower liquidity amplifying moves.

## Mechanistic Interpretation

Three invariances constrain the generative mechanism:

- **Time-invariance** → endogenous, not externally forced
- **Complement symmetry** → alignment-based, not direction-based
- **Timescale universality** → structural property of coupled oscillators

**Synthesis:** The regime cycle emerges from internal feedback between fast and slow trend processes. When all timescales align, the regime is stable. Fast-timescale misalignment initiates transition. The cycle period is set by the slow timescale; transition quality by the medium timescale's proximity to its own turning point.

**Quantitative signature (Phase 11):** The regime cycle is driven by sequential zero crossings across timescales. Fast trend crosses zero first → medium trend approaches zero → if medium crosses, transition succeeds. If medium retreats, transition fails. The logistic regression coefficients (trend_8h: 42.2 at C2, 48.1 at C1) measure proximity to a dynamical bifurcation point.

**Phase 12 confirmation:** 86.4% of OOS macro exits driven by trend_8h bit flip. The medium timescale is the primary regime-change driver. Model reads state quality (proximity to bifurcation), not predicting the future.

## Formulation Notes

The binary trigram framework was the right tool for **discovery** — finding the topology, proving universality, identifying symmetry. For **prediction**, logistic regression on continuous features (trend_1h + trend_8h) at regime exits captures the actionable signal.

**Operational architecture (OOS-validated):**
1. **Regime identification:** Binary trigram (trend_1h × trend_8h × trend_48h) → 4-regime directed cycle
2. **Exit quality prediction:** trend_8h magnitude at regime exit → P(favorable transition)
3. **Binary S5/S4 signal deprecated.** Use continuous trend_8h model.
4. **HMM:** Deferred pending operational evidence of binary boundary flickering.

## Caveats

- **Single asset.** No multi-asset validation yet. Topology may be BTC-specific.
- **Logistic coefficients are environment-dependent.** IS standardized coef 3.91 vs OOS 7.27 at C2. Same direction, different magnitude. Need refit on raw-scale data for deployment.
- **AUC partly mechanical.** 86% of exits are trend_8h bit flips. Model reads state quality, not predicting future. AUC overstates "prediction."
- **IS normalization unknown.** IS trends pre-normalized (std ~1) vs OOS raw (std ~1e-4). Cannot compare raw thresholds. Must refit on consistently-normalized data.
- **Calibration biases.** IS model overestimates extreme probabilities on OOS. Refit before using predicted probabilities.
- **S5→bull CI technically failed.** Point estimate 85.9% passes, lower CI 82.7% fails the >85% pre-commitment. Binary signal deprecated anyway.
- **C3→C0 crash untestable.** 1 crash total across IS+OOS (0 in OOS). Cannot model.
