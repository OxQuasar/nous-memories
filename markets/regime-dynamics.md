# Market Regime Dynamics

## Core Thesis — Final, March 2026

BTC intraday dynamics exhibit a **4-regime directed cycle** that is a **universal property of multi-timescale trend coupling**. The cycle is invariant under time-translation, direction-reversal, and timescale rescaling. It is generated by endogenous feedback between fast and slow trend oscillators, not by external calendar forcing.

**Confirmed across:** 6+ binary bases, 4 timescale ratios (1:4:16 through 1:16:96), 4 time-of-day blocks, 3 subperiods, trend and cross-channel feature sets.

### The Four-Regime Cycle

```
Bear (C0) → Reversal (C1) → Bull (C3) → Pullback (C2) → Bear (C0) → ...
```

Two coupled 2-cycles: {C0↔C1} bearish oscillation, {C2↔C3} bullish oscillation, weakly coupled by cross-macro transitions.

**Universality:** Identical topology in all 4 timescale bases tested (Phase 9). Same cycle, same forbidden transitions, same near-uniform stationary distribution (22–28%).

### Complement Symmetry (Coherence Parity)

The F₂³ complement involution (x ↦ x⊕7) is a symmetry of the empirical transition dynamics. **Basis-independent**: Basis A (JSD 0.051), Basis D (JSD < 0.09), Basis F (JSD 0.027), Basis G (JSD 0.034).

The market distinguishes *coherent* from *incoherent* states, not up from down. Coherent bear ≡ coherent bull under the transition structure.

### Structural Zeros

3 absolute zeros (C0↛C3, C1↛C2, C2↛C1) + 1 near-zero (C3→C0 at 0.13%). Hold perfectly across all time blocks, subperiods, and timescale bases. Stage-skipping is structurally forbidden.

### Time-Invariance

Transition TYPE is independent of time-of-day (all chi-squared p > 0.05). Transition TIMING shows mild non-uniformity (Asia 28.3% vs Evening 21.9%, p=0.038). The market changes regimes more often at certain hours, but *which* regime it transitions to is the same regardless of when.

## Mechanistic Interpretation

Three invariances constrain the generative mechanism:

1. **Time-invariance** → endogenous, not externally forced
2. **Complement symmetry** → alignment-based, not direction-based
3. **Timescale universality** → structural property of coupled oscillators, not timescale-specific

**Synthesis:** The regime cycle emerges from internal feedback between fast and slow trend processes. When all timescales align (C0 bear / C3 bull), the regime is stable. Misalignment on the fast timescale initiates transition (C1 reversal / C2 pullback). Cross-macro transitions occur when the medium timescale follows the fast timescale's lead. The cycle period is set by the slow timescale; the transition quality is determined by fast-medium coupling.
